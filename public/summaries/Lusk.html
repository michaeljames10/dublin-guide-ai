Welcome to Lusk: A Charming Village with a Rich History and a Vibrant Community

History

Steeped in history, Lusk dates back to the 5th century. It's home to the Round Tower and St. MacCullin's Church, both standing as proud reminders of our rich heritage. Over the years, Lusk has evolved from a quiet farming village to a bustling community, while still retaining its old-world charm.

Attractions

From the historic Lusk Heritage Centre to the beautiful beaches nearby, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the Round Tower, one of the finest of its kind in Ireland!

Restaurants & Pubs

For a small village, Lusk boasts a surprising number of top-notch eateries and cosy pubs. Locals recommend The Old School House for a hearty meal and The Strand Bar for a pint.

Green Spaces

Lusk is blessed with beautiful green spaces. Enjoy a leisurely stroll in Ballough Park or take a nature walk along the nearby coastline.

Local Life

Life in Lusk is laid-back and friendly. The community is tight-knit, with regular events and a strong sense of camaraderie. It's a place where everyone knows your name!

Schools

Lusk offers excellent primary and secondary schools, including Lusk Community College and Corduff National School. The area is also home to the renowned Rush and Lusk Educate Together National School.

Commuting & Transport

Getting around is easy with regular bus services to Dublin city centre. The village is also close to the M1 motorway and Dublin Airport. Plus, there's a great network of cycle paths and walkways for those who prefer to travel on foot or by bike.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Lusk was once home to Saint MacCullin, who was renowned for his healing powers? And don't forget to visit the secret garden at the back of the Round Tower - it's a real hidden gem!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its growing popularity, Lusk is definitely up-and-coming. There are plans for new residential developments, which will bring even more life and vibrancy to the area.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Lusk Strawberry Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Lusk. And let's not forget the local GAA matches - a real highlight of the sporting calendar!
