Welcome to Lusk, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

With roots dating back to the 5th century, Lusk is steeped in history. The area is named after Saint MacCullin, who founded a church here in 450 AD. Over the centuries, Lusk has evolved from a quiet farming village into a vibrant, modern community while still retaining its historical charm.

Attractions

From the ancient Lusk Round Tower to the modern Lusk Heritage Centre, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the beautiful beaches nearby, perfect for a leisurely stroll or a refreshing swim.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, locals recommend The Old School House or The Strand Bar. And for a pint, you can't beat the atmosphere at The Greyhound Inn.

Green Spaces

Lusk is blessed with plenty of green spaces. Check out Lusk Community Park for a picnic, or take a nature walk along the nearby coastline.

Local Life

Life in Lusk is relaxed and friendly, with a strong sense of community. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses are supported.

Schools

Lusk boasts several excellent primary and secondary schools, including Lusk Community College and Rush and Lusk Educate Together National School.

House Prices

House prices in Lusk are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it an attractive option for first-time buyers.

Commuting & Transport

Getting around is easy with regular bus services to Dublin city centre. Lusk is also close to the M1 motorway for those who prefer to drive.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Lusk was once home to the famous Irish pirate, Grace O'Malley? And don't miss the secret garden at the back of The Old School House.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new housing developments and improved transport links, Lusk is an area on the rise. But don't worry, it's not losing its charm anytime soon.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Lusk Strawberry Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Lusk.
