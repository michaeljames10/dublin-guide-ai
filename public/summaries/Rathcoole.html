Welcome to Rathcoole, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a quiet village, Rathcoole has evolved into a bustling suburban area while still retaining its historical charm. The area is steeped in history, with the Rathcoole Inn being one of the oldest pubs in Ireland.

Attractions

From the Avoca Rathcoole, the largest Avoca location in Ireland, to the Coolmine Equestrian Centre, there's plenty to see and do in Rathcoole.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty meal at An Poitin Stil or a pint at the Rathcoole Inn, there's a spot for everyone in Rathcoole.

Green Spaces

Rathcoole Park is a local favourite for a stroll or a picnic, and the nearby Corkagh Park Fishery is perfect for a spot of fishing.

Local Life

Life in Rathcoole is a blend of the traditional and the modern, with a strong sense of community and a vibrant local culture.

Schools

Rathcoole is home to several excellent schools, including Holy Family National School and Scoil Chrónáin.

House Prices

House prices in Rathcoole are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a mix of traditional and modern homes available.

Rent Prices

Rents in Rathcoole are slightly below the Dublin average, making it a more affordable option. Typical rents are as follows:

  • 1-bed: €1,200
  • 2-bed: €1,500
  • 3-bed: €1,800

Crime Index

Rathcoole is generally a safe area, with a low crime index.

Commuting & Transport

With regular bus services and close proximity to the Luas Green Line, commuting from Rathcoole is a breeze. The area is also very walkable and cyclist-friendly.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Don't miss the annual Rathcoole Festival, a local tradition that brings the community together with music, food, and fun.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With ongoing development and a growing population, Rathcoole is an area on the rise, while still maintaining its unique charm.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers market to the annual Christmas fair, there's always something happening in Rathcoole.
