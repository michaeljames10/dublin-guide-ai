Welcome to Leopardstown: Your Local Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Leopardstown, named after a local tavern, has a rich history dating back to the 13th century. It's evolved from a quiet rural area to a bustling suburb, with a unique blend of old and new.

Attractions

From the famous Leopardstown Racecourse to the Central Park business district, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the local farmers' market every Friday!

Restaurants & Pubs

Leopardstown boasts a variety of eateries and pubs. Locals recommend The Leopardstown Inn for a pint and The Gables for a hearty meal.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at Leopardstown Park, or take a nature walk along the nearby Wicklow Way. Plenty of green spaces for a breath of fresh air!

Local Life

Leopardstown is a vibrant community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. It's a great place to live, with a mix of families, professionals, and students.

Schools

There are several excellent primary and secondary schools in the area, including St. Kilian's German School and Holy Trinity National School.

House Prices

House prices in Leopardstown are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a mix of apartments, townhouses, and detached homes.

Commuting & Transport

Leopardstown is well-served by public transport, with the Luas Green Line, several bus routes, and cycleways. It's about a 30-minute commute to the city centre.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Check out the Leopardstown Christmas Festival, a local tradition since 1888. And don't miss the secret garden at the end of Burton Hall Road!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Leopardstown is a growing area with several new developments planned, but it's also committed to preserving its unique character and charm.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers' market to the annual Christmas festival, there's always something happening in Leopardstown.
