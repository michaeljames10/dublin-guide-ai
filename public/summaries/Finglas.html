Welcome to Finglas: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Northside Gem

History

From its humble beginnings as a small rural village, Finglas has grown into a bustling urban neighbourhood. It's steeped in history, with roots dating back to the Norman invasion. The area is home to the ancient Finglas Abbey and the iconic Tolka Valley Park, which has been a local favourite for centuries.

Attractions

There's never a dull moment in Finglas. You can explore the National Botanic Gardens, catch a show at the Axis Theatre, or take a stroll down the historic Main Street. And don't forget to check out the famous John Paul Park, affectionately known as 'The Bogies' by locals.

Restaurants & Pubs

From traditional Irish pubs to trendy eateries, Finglas has it all. Locals recommend The Full Shilling for a pint and The Washerwoman for a hearty meal. For a taste of the exotic, try the Thai dishes at Camile.

Green Spaces

Finglas is blessed with plenty of green spaces. Glasnevin Cemetery offers tranquil walks amidst historic monuments, while the nearby Tolka Valley Park is perfect for a family picnic or a jog along the river.

Local Life

Life in Finglas is a blend of old and new. It's a close-knit community with a vibrant local culture, where neighbours know each other by name and local businesses thrive. The area is known for its strong sporting tradition, particularly in Gaelic games.

Schools

Finglas boasts a number of excellent primary and secondary schools, including Saint Brigid's and Beneavin College. The area is also home to the innovative Finglas Training Centre, which offers a range of vocational courses.

House Prices

House prices in Finglas are generally more affordable than the Dublin average, with a mix of traditional terraced houses and modern apartments on offer. The area has seen steady growth in recent years, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Finglas are relatively affordable, with the average rent for a 1-bed apartment around €1,200, a 2-bed place for €1,500, and a 3-bed home for €1,800. Compared to the Dublin average, Finglas offers good value for money. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Finglas has seen significant improvements in public safety in recent years. The area has a strong community spirit, which contributes to its safety rating of 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪.

Commuting & Transport

Getting around Finglas is a breeze, with regular bus services to the city centre and beyond. The area is also well-served by cycle lanes and pedestrian-friendly streets. The city centre is just a 20-minute drive away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the legendary 'Finglas Elvis' to the secret garden at the end of Seamus Ennis Road, Finglas is full of surprises. And let's not forget the annual Finglas Festival, a celebration of local culture and community spirit.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With ongoing regeneration projects and a growing population, Finglas is definitely on the up. Yet, it retains its unique charm and community spirit, making it a great place to live, work and play.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers' market to the annual Finglas Festival, there's always something happening in Finglas. The area is also home to a vibrant arts scene, with regular exhibitions and performances at the Axis Theatre.
