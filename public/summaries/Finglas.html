Welcome to Finglas: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Northside Gem

History

From its humble beginnings as a small rural village, Finglas has grown into a bustling urban neighbourhood. It's steeped in history, with roots dating back to the Norman invasion. The area is home to several historic sites, including the ancient Finglas Abbey and the iconic Tolka Valley Park.

Attractions

There's plenty to see and do in Finglas. Visit the National Botanic Gardens, explore the Glasnevin Cemetery Museum, or take a stroll along the Royal Canal. For the thrill-seekers, there's the adrenaline-pumping Finglas Adventure Centre.

Restaurants & Pubs

From traditional Irish pubs to trendy eateries, Finglas has it all. Locals recommend The Grave Digger's Pub for a pint and The Full Shilling for a hearty meal. For a taste of the exotic, try the delicious Indian cuisine at Bombay Pantry.

Green Spaces

Finglas is blessed with beautiful parks and green spaces. Tolka Valley Park is a local favourite, offering scenic walking trails and a peaceful retreat from the city buzz. Johnstown Park is another gem, with its well-maintained sports facilities and playgrounds.

Local Life

Life in Finglas is a blend of old and new, with a strong sense of community and a vibrant local culture. The area is known for its friendly locals, lively arts scene, and rich sporting heritage.

Schools

Finglas boasts a range of quality primary and secondary schools. Notable institutions include Saint Brigid's Senior Girls' National School and Beneavin De La Salle College.

House Prices

House prices in Finglas are generally more affordable than the Dublin average, with a mix of traditional and modern homes available.

Commuting & Transport

Getting around Finglas is easy, with regular bus services, cycleways, and walkable streets. The city centre is just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the legendary Finglas Festival to the hidden gem that is the Lanesborough Park, Finglas is full of surprises. Famous past residents include Thin Lizzy frontman Phil Lynott and U2's Larry Mullen Jr.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Finglas is an up-and-coming area, with several new developments in the pipeline. Yet, it retains its charming local character and community spirit.

Local Events & Markets

Finglas is known for its lively local events, from the annual Finglas Festival to the weekly farmers' market. The area also has a strong sporting tradition, with several local GAA clubs.
