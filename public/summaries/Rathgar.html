Welcome to Rathgar: A Charming Dublin Suburb with a Rich History and Vibrant Local Life

History

Once a quiet village on the outskirts of Dublin, Rathgar has grown into a bustling suburb while retaining its historical charm. Notable for its Victorian and Edwardian houses, Rathgar was the birthplace of renowned author James Joyce.

Attractions

From the James Joyce Tower to the beautiful St. Patrick's Cathedral, Rathgar is full of interesting spots. Don't miss the local shops on Rathgar Road, each with its own unique character.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of Guinness, Rathgar's pubs and restaurants have you covered. Locals recommend The 108 for its cosy atmosphere and excellent food.

Green Spaces

Rathgar is home to several parks and green spaces, perfect for a leisurely stroll or a picnic. Bushy Park, with its beautiful woodland and river walks, is a local favourite.

Local Life

Living in Rathgar is like being part of a close-knit community. The area has a relaxed, friendly vibe, with plenty of local events and activities to get involved in.

Schools

Rathgar boasts a number of excellent primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded Rathgar Junior School and The High School.

House Prices

House prices in Rathgar tend to be higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and excellent amenities. Typical prices for a home range from €600,000 to over €1 million.

Rent Prices

Rents in Rathgar are also above the Dublin average, with typical rents for a 1-bed, 2-bed, and 3-bed places at €1,500, €2,000, and €2,500 respectively. Affordability compared to the Dublin average: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Rathgar is considered a safe area, with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular buses to the city centre and easy access to the Luas and Dart, getting around from Rathgar is a breeze. The area is also very walkable, with plenty of cycleways for those who prefer to travel on two wheels.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Rathgar was the birthplace of Bram Stoker, author of Dracula? Or that the area is home to a secret garden, hidden away behind the local library? Discover these and more local legends when you explore Rathgar.

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Rathgar has seen some development in recent years, the area has largely retained its charming, village-like atmosphere. The future looks bright for this desirable Dublin suburb.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Rathgar Horticultural Show to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Rathgar. The area also hosts a number of sports clubs, including rugby, cricket, and tennis.

Area Vibe Score
