Welcome to Crumlin, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a rural village, Crumlin has evolved into a bustling Dublin suburb. It's steeped in history, with roots dating back to the 12th century. The area is named after the Irish word 'Cromghlinn', meaning 'crooked glen'.

Attractions

From the historic Crumlin Road Gaol to the bustling Crumlin Shopping Centre, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss a visit to the childhood home of U2's frontman, Bono!

Restaurants & Pubs

For a pint and a chat, head to The Marble Arch. If you're peckish, try the local favourite, The Hungry Duck. The locals swear by their brunch!

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll in Sundrive Park or take the kids to the playground in Brickfield Park. Crumlin is also home to the beautiful Pearse Park.

Local Life

Crumlin is a close-knit community with a friendly vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in the area, including the well-regarded St. Agnes' Primary School and Crumlin College of Further Education.

House Prices

House prices in Crumlin are generally more affordable than the Dublin average, with a mix of terraced houses and semi-detached homes.

Commuting & Transport

With several bus routes and close proximity to the Luas Red Line, commuting is a breeze. The city centre is just a 15-minute drive away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Crumlin is the birthplace of several famous musicians, including Bono and Brendan Behan? And don't miss the secret garden at the Crumlin Bowling Club!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its affordable housing and convenient location, Crumlin is an up-and-coming area. Several new developments are in the pipeline, promising to add even more to this vibrant community.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Crumlin Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Crumlin.
