Welcome to Crumlin, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a rural village, Crumlin has evolved into a bustling Dublin suburb. It's steeped in history, with roots dating back to the 12th century. The area is named after the Irish word 'Cromghlinn', meaning 'crooked glen'.

Attractions

From the historic Crumlin Road Gaol to the Drimnagh Castle, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the Pearse Lyons Distillery, a whiskey lover's paradise.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a hearty meal, head to The Hungry Duck. If you fancy a pint, The Marble Arch is a local favourite.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll in Sundrive Park or take a nature walk along the Grand Canal.

Local Life

Crumlin is a tight-knit community with a friendly vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in the area, including the well-regarded St. Agnes' Primary School.

House Prices

House prices in Crumlin are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.

Rent Prices

Rents are also more affordable than the city average. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed place.

💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪ Affordability

Crime Index

Crumlin is generally safe, with a crime index lower than the city average.

🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪ Safety

Commuting & Transport

With regular bus services and cycleways, getting around is easy. The city centre is just a 15-minute drive away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Crumlin is the birthplace of U2's frontman, Bono? And don't miss the secret garden at the end of Windmill Road.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new developments and regeneration projects, Crumlin is an area on the rise.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Crumlin Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening.
