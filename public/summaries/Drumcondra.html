Welcome to Drumcondra: Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Drumcondra's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From its origins as a Viking settlement to its growth during the Georgian era, this area has seen it all. The historic Drumcondra Castle and the iconic Croke Park Stadium are testaments to its vibrant past.

Attractions

From the National Botanic Gardens to the Tolka Park, Drumcondra is a treasure trove of attractions. Don't miss the chance to catch a Gaelic football match at Croke Park or a stroll along the River Tolka.

Restaurants & Pubs

Drumcondra is a foodie's paradise. From the traditional Irish fare at Fagan's to the exotic flavours at Shouk, there's something for everyone. And for a pint? You can't beat Quinn's.

Green Spaces

With the National Botanic Gardens and Griffith Park, Drumcondra is a green oasis in the heart of Dublin. Perfect for a picnic, a jog, or a leisurely walk.

Local Life

Drumcondra is a vibrant, friendly neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It's a place where locals chat in the street and children play in the parks. It's Dublin, but with a village feel.

Schools

Drumcondra is home to some of Dublin's top schools, including Drumcondra National School and St. Patrick's College. It's also the location of the Marino Institute of Education, a leading teacher training institution.

House Prices

House prices in Drumcondra are generally above the Dublin average, reflecting the area's popularity and amenities. Expect to pay a premium for Georgian and Victorian properties.

Rent Prices

Rents in Drumcondra are moderate by Dublin standards. Expect to pay around €1,500 for a 1-bed, €1,800 for a 2-bed, and €2,200 for a 3-bed. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Drumcondra is generally safe, with a crime index below the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Drumcondra is well served by public transport, with regular bus services and a train station. It's also a great area for cycling and walking, with the city centre just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the secret garden at the Archbishop's House to the legendary gigs at the Cat and Cage, Drumcondra is full of surprises. And did you know that James Joyce once lived here?

Property Trends & Future Developments

Drumcondra is a sought-after area with a strong property market. With ongoing regeneration and new developments, it's an area on the up.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Drumcondra Village Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Drumcondra.
