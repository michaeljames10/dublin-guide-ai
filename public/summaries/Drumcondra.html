Welcome to Drumcondra: Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Drumcondra's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From its origins as a Viking settlement to its growth during the Georgian era, this area has seen it all. The historic Drumcondra Castle and the iconic Croke Park Stadium are testaments to its storied past.

Attractions

From the National Botanic Gardens to the Tolka Park, Drumcondra is brimming with attractions. Don't miss the chance to catch a Gaelic football match at Croke Park or take a stroll along the River Tolka.

Restaurants & Pubs

Drumcondra is a foodie's paradise. Check out Fagans for a traditional Irish pub experience or head to Shouk for some of the best Middle Eastern cuisine in Dublin. And don't forget to grab a pint at The Ivy House.

Green Spaces

With the National Botanic Gardens and Griffith Park, Drumcondra is a green oasis in the heart of Dublin. Whether you're a jogger, a dog walker, or just a nature lover, you'll find plenty to enjoy here.

Local Life

Drumcondra is a vibrant, friendly neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It's a place where locals chat in the street and children play in the parks. It's Dublin, but with a village feel.

Schools

Drumcondra is home to some of Dublin's top schools, including St. Patrick's College and Drumcondra National School. It's also close to Dublin City University, making it a great place for families and students alike.

House Prices

House prices in Drumcondra are generally above the Dublin average, reflecting the area's popularity and amenities. Expect to pay a premium for Georgian and Victorian properties.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Drumcondra are moderate compared to the rest of Dublin. A 1-bed apartment typically costs around €1,500 per month, while a 3-bed house can be around €2,500. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Drumcondra is generally a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular bus services, close proximity to the city centre, and plenty of cycle lanes, getting around Drumcondra is a breeze. And with the M1 motorway nearby, you're well connected to the rest of Ireland.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bram Stoker, author of Dracula, was born in Drumcondra? Or that the area is home to the oldest tree in Dublin? There's always something new to discover in Drumcondra.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Drumcondra is a sought-after area that's only getting more popular. With new developments and renovations, it's an area on the rise, but it still retains its historic charm.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Drumcondra Village Market to the Croke Park concerts, there's always something happening in Drumcondra. And don't miss the local GAA matches – they're a real slice of Dublin life.
