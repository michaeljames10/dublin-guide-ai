Welcome to Malahide: A Charming Coastal Village with a Rich History

History

Malahide, a picturesque seaside town, has a rich history dating back to the 12th century. It's home to the magnificent Malahide Castle, which was owned by the Talbot family for nearly 800 years. The village has evolved over the centuries, maintaining its historic charm while embracing modern amenities.

Attractions

From Malahide Castle and its beautiful gardens to the bustling marina and sandy beaches, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the unique shops in the village centre and the stunning coastal walks.

Restaurants & Pubs

Enjoy a pint at Gibney's, a traditional Irish pub, or dine at one of the many top-rated restaurants like Nautilus or Bon Appetit. Seafood lovers will be in heaven here!

Green Spaces

Malahide boasts numerous parks and green spaces. The Malahide Castle grounds are perfect for a leisurely stroll, and the coastal walks offer breathtaking views. For the more adventurous, there's also kite surfing at the beach.

Local Life

Living in Malahide is like being on holiday all year round. The community is friendly and welcoming, and there's always something happening, from local festivals to live music in the pubs.

Schools

Malahide has a number of excellent primary and secondary schools, including Malahide Community School and Pobalscoil Iosolde. There's also a wide range of sports clubs for kids.

Commuting & Transport

With a DART station in the village centre, commuting to Dublin city centre is a breeze. There's also a good bus service, and the village is very walkable.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Check out the secret garden at the back of the village library, or try to spot the ghost of Malahide Castle. And don't forget to ask a local about the legend of the White Lady!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Malahide has seen some new development in recent years, the village has managed to retain its unique character and charm.
