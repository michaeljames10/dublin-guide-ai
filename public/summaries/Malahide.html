Welcome to Malahide: A Charming Coastal Village with a Rich History and Vibrant Local Life

History

Malahide has a rich history dating back to the 12th century. It's home to the magnificent Malahide Castle, which has stood for over 800 years. The village has evolved from a quiet fishing village to a bustling coastal town, while still retaining its historical charm.

Attractions

From the stunning Malahide Castle and Gardens to the picturesque marina, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the Talbot Botanic Gardens and the local cricket club, a favourite among locals.

Restaurants & Pubs

Enjoy a pint at Gibney's, a local institution, or dine at one of the many seafood restaurants like Fish Shack Café. For a special treat, try the Michelin-starred Bon Appétit.

Green Spaces

Malahide boasts numerous parks and green spaces. The village is surrounded by natural beauty, from the coastal walks along the beach to the lush Malahide Demesne.

Local Life

Life in Malahide is relaxed and community-focused. The village has a friendly, welcoming vibe with a strong sense of local pride. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

Malahide has a number of excellent primary and secondary schools, including Malahide Community School and St. Oliver Plunkett's National School. The area is also home to the renowned Portmarnock Community School.

House Prices

House prices in Malahide are generally higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability. Expect to pay a premium for properties with sea views or proximity to the village centre.

Rent Prices

Rents in Malahide are also above the Dublin average. A one-bedroom apartment typically costs around €1,500 per month, while a three-bedroom house can be upwards of €2,500. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Malahide is considered a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular DART and bus services, commuting to Dublin city centre is easy. The village is also very walkable and has a network of cycle paths.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Discover the secret garden at the end of the West Pier, or try to spot the ghost of Malahide Castle. And don't forget to check out the annual Malahide Has It festival!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Malahide has seen some modern development, it remains committed to preserving its historical charm and village atmosphere.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers market to the annual cricket festival, there's always something happening in Malahide.
