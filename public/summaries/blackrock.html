Welcome to Blackrock, Dublin's Seaside Gem

History

Blackrock's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From its origins as a small fishing village to its evolution into a bustling seaside suburb, Blackrock has always been a place of charm and character.

Attractions

From the historic Blackrock Castle to the bustling Blackrock Market, there's plenty to see and do in this vibrant neighbourhood.

Restaurants & Pubs

Blackrock is home to some of Dublin's best eateries and watering holes. Locals recommend the seafood at Ouzos and a pint at the Breffni Inn.

Green Spaces

Blackrock Park is a local favourite for a stroll or picnic, and the coastline offers stunning views and walks.

Local Life

Blackrock has a friendly, community vibe with a mix of families, professionals, and retirees. It's a place where people know their neighbours and local shopkeepers.

Schools

Blackrock boasts excellent schools, including Blackrock College and St. Andrew's College, both renowned for their high academic standards.

House Prices

House prices in Blackrock tend to be higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and amenities.

Rent Prices

Rents in Blackrock are also above the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,500 for a 1-bed, €2,000 for a 2-bed, and €2,500+ for a 3-bed. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Blackrock is generally safe and peaceful, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Blackrock is well-served by public transport, including the DART and several bus routes. It's also a great area for cycling and walking.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Don't miss the secret garden at the Blackrock Library, and be sure to ask locals about the legend of the Blackrock Dolmen.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Blackrock continues to be a sought-after area, with ongoing improvements to amenities and infrastructure.

Local Events & Markets

Check out the weekly farmers' market and the annual Blackrock Film Festival.
