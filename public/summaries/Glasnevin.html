Welcome to Glasnevin: Dublin's Historic and Vibrant Neighbourhood

History

Known for its rich history, Glasnevin is home to many key events and heritage sites. From the establishment of the Glasnevin Cemetery in 1832 to the birthplace of many Irish revolutionaries, the area has evolved into a bustling neighbourhood with a deep-rooted history.

Attractions

There's no shortage of things to do in Glasnevin. Visit the National Botanic Gardens, explore the Glasnevin Cemetery Museum, or take a stroll along the River Tolka. Don't forget to check out the Brian Boru pub, a local landmark!

Restaurants & Pubs

From traditional Irish pubs to modern eateries, Glasnevin has it all. Locals recommend The Gravediggers Pub for a pint and The Washerwoman for a hearty meal.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at the National Botanic Gardens or take a nature walk along the River Tolka. Glasnevin is also home to several parks, perfect for a family picnic or a leisurely stroll.

Local Life

Glasnevin is a vibrant community with a friendly vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive. The area is known for its lively arts scene and community events.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in Glasnevin, including the notable St. Vincent's CBS. The area is also home to the Dublin City University, making it a hub for students.
