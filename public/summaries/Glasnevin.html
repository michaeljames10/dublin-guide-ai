Welcome to Glasnevin: Dublin's Historic and Vibrant Neighbourhood

History

Known for its rich history, Glasnevin is home to many key events in Irish history. From the establishment of the Glasnevin Cemetery in 1832 to the birthplace of many Irish revolutionaries, the area has evolved into a bustling neighbourhood while preserving its heritage.

Attractions

Don't miss the National Botanic Gardens, Glasnevin Cemetery Museum, and the Gravediggers Pub. There's always something interesting to see in Glasnevin!

Restaurants & Pubs

From the traditional Irish fare at The Gravediggers Pub to the modern cuisine at Anderson's Creperie, Glasnevin offers a variety of dining options. Locals recommend the Sunday roast at The Washerwoman.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll in the National Botanic Gardens or a picnic in Albert College Park. Glasnevin is a green oasis in the heart of Dublin.

Local Life

Glasnevin is a vibrant community with a friendly vibe. It's a place where history meets modern life, and where locals enjoy a high quality of life.

Schools

Home to several primary and secondary schools, Glasnevin is also the location of the renowned Dublin City University.

House Prices

House prices in Glasnevin are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a mix of period homes and modern apartments.

Commuting & Transport

With several bus routes, cycleways, and walkable streets, getting around Glasnevin is easy. The city centre is just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the secret garden in the Botanic Gardens to the legendary ghost stories of the Gravediggers Pub, Glasnevin is full of surprises.
