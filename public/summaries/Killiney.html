Welcome to Killiney, Dublin's Coastal Gem

History

Once a quiet fishing village, Killiney has evolved into a posh suburb, known for its stunning sea views and celebrity residents. Its history is rich with Viking invasions, Victorian grandeur, and a vibrant arts scene.

Attractions

From the panoramic views atop Killiney Hill to the historic Killiney Castle, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the beautiful Killiney Beach, perfect for a refreshing dip or a leisurely stroll.

Restaurants & Pubs

Enjoy a pint at the local pub, The Druid's Chair, or indulge in gourmet dining at the Michelin-starred DeVille's. For a casual bite, the Beach House Bistro is a local favourite.

Green Spaces

With Killiney Hill Park, Victoria Park, and numerous coastal walks, Killiney is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. The area is also home to several golf clubs.

Local Life

Living in Killiney is like being on a permanent holiday. The community is friendly and laid-back, with a love for the arts and the great outdoors.

Schools

Killiney boasts excellent schools, including the highly-regarded Holy Child Killiney and St. Joseph of Cluny. The area is also home to the innovative IADT Dun Laoghaire.

House Prices

With its stunning views and desirable location, Killiney is one of Dublin's priciest areas. Expect to pay a premium for the privilege of calling this coastal gem home.

Rent Prices

Rents in Killiney are higher than the Dublin average, with a 1-bed flat typically costing around €1,500 per month. Despite the high costs, many find the area's charm and beauty worth the price. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Killiney is generally safe, with a lower crime rate than the city centre. However, like any area, it's always wise to stay vigilant. Safety: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With the DART station and numerous bus routes, getting around is a breeze. The area is also very walkable, with many residents choosing to cycle or stroll to their destinations.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bono lives in Killiney? Or that the area is home to a secret tunnel network? Discover these and more local legends as you explore the area.

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Killiney has seen some modern development, it retains its historic charm. The area continues to attract those seeking a peaceful coastal lifestyle within easy reach of the city.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Killiney Arts Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening. The area is also known for its vibrant music scene, with regular gigs and concerts.
