Welcome to Killiney, Dublin's Coastal Gem

History

Once a quiet fishing village, Killiney has evolved into a posh suburb, known for its stunning sea views and celebrity residents. Its history is rich with Viking invasions, Victorian grandeur, and a vibrant arts scene.

Attractions

From the panoramic views atop Killiney Hill to the historic Killiney Castle, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the beautiful Killiney Beach, perfect for a refreshing dip or a leisurely stroll.

Restaurants & Pubs

Enjoy a pint at the local pub, The Druid's Chair, or indulge in gourmet dining at the Michelin-starred DeVille's. For a casual bite, the Beach House Bistro is a local favourite.

Green Spaces

With Killiney Hill Park and the nearby Wicklow Mountains, Killiney is a haven for nature lovers. There are plenty of trails for hiking, biking, and bird-watching.

Local Life

Living in Killiney is like being on a permanent holiday. The community is friendly and laid-back, with a love for the outdoors and a strong sense of local pride.

Schools

Killiney boasts excellent schools, including the highly-regarded Holy Child Killiney and St. Joseph of Cluny. The area is also home to the innovative IADT Dun Laoghaire.

House Prices

With its luxury homes and sea views, Killiney is one of Dublin's priciest areas. Expect to pay a premium for the privilege of living here.

Rent Prices

Rents in Killiney are higher than the Dublin average, with a 1-bed flat costing around €1,500 per month. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Killiney is generally safe, with a low crime rate. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With the DART train and several bus routes, getting around is easy. The city centre is just a 30-minute commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bono lives in Killiney? Or that the beach was a favourite spot of James Joyce? Discover these and other local secrets.

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Killiney retains its historic charm, new developments are adding a modern touch. The area continues to attract those seeking a luxurious coastal lifestyle.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Killiney Arts Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening. And don't miss the traditional Christmas swim at the beach!
