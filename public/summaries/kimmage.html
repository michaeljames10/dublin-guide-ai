Welcome to Kimmage: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a rural village, Kimmage has evolved into a vibrant suburb of Dublin. Its history is rich with tales of rebellion, resilience, and community spirit. The area is known for its role in the 1916 Easter Rising, with many local landmarks commemorating this pivotal event in Irish history.

Attractions

From the historic Kimmage Manor to the bustling Sundrive Shopping Centre, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the local art scene at the Kimmage Craft Market, and be sure to catch a match at the Carlisle Grounds, home to the local football club.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of the black stuff, Kimmage has you covered. Locals recommend The Kimmage Arms for its cosy atmosphere and traditional fare, while the Sundrive Cafe is a favourite for brunch.

Green Spaces

Despite its urban setting, Kimmage is blessed with plenty of green spaces. Take a stroll through the leafy Tymon Park, or enjoy a picnic in the picturesque Kimmage Manor grounds.

Local Life

Kimmage is a tight-knit community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name, and local businesses are supported with pride. The area is diverse and inclusive, with a mix of families, young professionals, and retirees.

Schools

Kimmage is home to several excellent primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded Kimmage Manor Primary and St. Kevin's College. The area also boasts a number of adult education centres and language schools.

House Prices

House prices in Kimmage are generally more affordable than in central Dublin, with a mix of period homes and modern apartments. The average house price is around €350,000, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Kimmage are relatively affordable compared to the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Kimmage is generally a safe area, with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular bus services, cycle lanes, and pedestrian-friendly streets, getting around Kimmage is a breeze. The city centre is just a 20-minute bus ride away, making it a popular choice for commuters.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the secret garden at Kimmage Manor to the legendary ghost of the Carlisle Grounds, Kimmage is full of surprises. And don't forget to try the famous Kimmage Scone at the Sundrive Cafe!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its affordable housing, excellent amenities, and friendly community, Kimmage is an up-and-coming area. Future developments include a new shopping centre and a community arts hub.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly Kimmage Craft Market to the annual Kimmage Festival, there's always something happening. Sports fans will love the local GAA matches, while foodies will be spoilt for choice at the monthly food market.
