Welcome to Milltown, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Milltown's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. Once a bustling mill village, it's now a charming residential area with a unique blend of old and new. The Dodder River, which powered the mills, still flows through, adding a touch of nature to our urban setting.

Attractions

From the historic Mount Saint Annes to the modern Frascati Shopping Centre, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the Nine Arches Viaduct, a local landmark that's as sturdy as a Dubliner's resolve.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, try Wilde & Green. For a pint, you can't beat The Dropping Well, a pub that's been serving locals since 1847. It's as Dublin as a bowl of coddle.

Green Spaces

Milltown Park is a local favourite for a stroll or a kickabout. The Dodder River Walk is another great spot for a bit of fresh air and a gander at the local wildlife.

Local Life

Life in Milltown is laid-back and friendly. It's a close-knit community where neighbours chat over the garden fence and local kids play on the green. It's Dublin, but with a village vibe.

Schools

Milltown is home to several top-notch schools, including Alexandra College and Gonzaga College. For the little ones, there's Milltown Parish Primary School.

House Prices

House prices in Milltown are a bit above the Dublin average, but you're paying for a prime location and a great community. It's worth every penny.

Commuting & Transport

Milltown is well-served by the Luas Green Line and several bus routes. It's also a great area for cycling and walking. City centre is just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Milltown was once home to a famous ghost? The White Lady of Milltown was a local legend. And don't miss the secret garden at Mount Saint Annes. It's a real hidden gem.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Milltown is a stable and desirable area. It's not changing much, and that's just how we like it.

Local Events & Markets

Check out the Milltown Market for local produce and crafts. And don't miss the annual Milltown Festival, a real community event.
