Welcome to Goatstown, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Goatstown, or 'Baile na nGabhar' in Irish, has a rich history dating back to the 18th century. Originally a small rural village, it has evolved into a bustling suburban area while still retaining its charming heritage.

Attractions

From the historic Goatstown Cross to the bustling Dundrum Town Centre, there's plenty to see and do in Goatstown. Don't miss the local farmers market every Saturday!

Restaurants & Pubs

Goatstown is home to some of Dublin's best eateries and pubs. Locals recommend The Goat Grill for a hearty meal and The Goatstown Inn for a pint of Guinness.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at Deerpark, a local favourite for nature walks and picnics. The UCD Rosemount Environmental Research Station is also a must-visit for nature enthusiasts.

Local Life

Goatstown has a friendly, community-oriented vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

Goatstown is home to several excellent primary and secondary schools, including Our Lady's Grove and Mount Anville.

House Prices

House prices in Goatstown are generally above the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and excellent amenities.

Rent Prices

Rents in Goatstown are also above average, with typical rents for 1-bed, 2-bed, and 3-bed places at €1,500, €2,000, and €2,500 respectively. Affordability compared to the Dublin average: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Commuting & Transport

Goatstown is well-served by public transport, including the Luas and several bus routes. It's also a great area for cycling and walking, with the city centre just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Goatstown was once home to famous Irish writer James Joyce? And don't miss the secret garden at the end of Farmhill Road!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Goatstown is a sought-after area with steady property values. It's a great place to invest, with several exciting developments planned for the future.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Goatstown Christmas Fair to the weekly farmers market, there's always something happening in Goatstown.
