Welcome to Goatstown, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Goatstown, or Baile na nGabhar in Irish, has a rich history dating back to the 18th century. Originally a small rural village, it has evolved into a bustling suburban area while still retaining its charming heritage.

Attractions

From the iconic Goatstown Cross to the historic Roebuck Castle, there's plenty to see and do in Goatstown. Don't miss the local farmers market every Saturday!

Restaurants & Pubs

Goatstown is home to some of Dublin's best eateries and pubs. Locals recommend The Goat Grill for a hearty meal and The Goatstown Inn for a pint of Guinness.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll in Deerpark, a beautiful green space perfect for picnics and nature walks. The park also hosts outdoor yoga classes during the summer.

Local Life

Living in Goatstown is like being part of a tight-knit community. The area has a friendly, welcoming vibe and is known for its annual street festivals.

Schools

Goatstown boasts excellent primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded Our Lady's Grove schools.

House Prices

House prices in Goatstown are generally on the higher side, reflecting the area's desirability and excellent amenities.

Rent Prices

Rents in Goatstown are slightly above the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,500 for a 1-bed, €2,000 for a 2-bed, and €2,500 for a 3-bed place. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Goatstown is a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Goatstown is well-served by Luas, Dart, and buses. It's also a great area for cycling and walking, with the city centre just a 30-minute commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Goatstown was once home to famous Irish writer James Joyce? And don't miss the secret garden behind Roebuck Castle!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Goatstown is a stable area with steady property values. There are plans for further development of green spaces and community facilities.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Goatstown Street Festival to the weekly farmers market, there's always something happening in Goatstown.
