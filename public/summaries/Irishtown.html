Welcome to Irishtown: Your Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a small village outside the city walls, Irishtown has grown into a vibrant neighbourhood with a rich history. From its roots as a refuge for the city's medieval outcasts, to its role in the 1916 Rising, Irishtown's past is as colourful as its present.

Attractions

From the iconic Poolbeg Lighthouse to the historic Ringsend Park, there's plenty to see and do in Irishtown. Don't miss the Sandymount Strand, a beautiful beach that's perfect for a leisurely stroll.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of the black stuff, Irishtown's got you covered. Locals recommend Slattery's for a traditional pub experience, and the Vintage Kitchen for a top-notch meal.

Green Spaces

Irishtown is home to several lovely parks, including the Irishtown Nature Park and Ringsend Park. Whether you're into birdwatching, picnicking, or just enjoying the great outdoors, you'll find plenty to love here.

Local Life

Life in Irishtown is laid-back and friendly, with a strong sense of community. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name, and where local traditions are kept alive and well.

Schools

Irishtown boasts several excellent schools, including the highly-regarded St. Matthew's Primary School and Ringsend College. The area is also home to the Irishtown Community Centre, which offers a range of classes and activities for all ages.

House Prices

House prices in Irishtown are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a typical home costing around €400,000. However, prices can vary widely depending on the property's size, condition, and location.

Rent Prices

Rents in Irishtown are relatively affordable, with a typical 1-bed flat going for around €1,200 per month, a 2-bed for €1,600, and a 3-bed for €2,000. Compared to the Dublin average, Irishtown offers good value for money. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Irishtown is generally a safe place to live, with a crime index that's lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Getting around Irishtown is a breeze, thanks to its excellent public transport links. The area is served by several bus routes, and the nearby Grand Canal Dock DART station offers easy access to the city centre.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the legendary "Ghost of Irishtown" to the secret tunnel under Ringsend Park, Irishtown is full of surprises. And don't forget to check out the local tradition of "Wren Day" on St. Stephen's Day!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its convenient location and friendly vibe, Irishtown is an increasingly popular place to live. The area is currently undergoing a period of regeneration, with several new developments in the pipeline.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Irishtown Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something going on in Irishtown. And don't miss the local GAA matches, a true Irish sporting tradition!
