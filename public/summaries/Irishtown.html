Welcome to Irishtown: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a medieval suburb, Irishtown has a rich history that's as colourful as its vibrant streets. From its origins as a settlement for the Vikings, to its role in the 1916 Rising, Irishtown's past is woven into the fabric of Dublin's history.

Attractions

From the iconic Poolbeg Lighthouse to the historic Ringsend Park, there's no shortage of sights to see in Irishtown. Don't miss the chance to explore the Sandymount Strand, a local favourite for a leisurely stroll.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of the black stuff, Irishtown's got you covered. Check out The Old Spot for some top-notch grub, or head to The Bath Pub for a traditional Irish pub experience.

Green Spaces

With its close proximity to the coast, Irishtown is a haven for nature lovers. The Sean Moore Park and Irishtown Nature Park offer plenty of green spaces for a picnic or a jog.

Local Life

Irishtown is a tight-knit community with a laid-back vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name, and where local traditions are kept alive and well.

Schools

Home to several primary and secondary schools, Irishtown offers a range of educational options. The area is also close to several universities, making it a popular choice for students.

Commuting & Transport

With several bus routes and a nearby DART station, getting around Irishtown is a breeze. The area is also highly walkable, with most amenities within a short stroll.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the legendary "Ghost of Irishtown" to the secret garden at the end of Bath Avenue, Irishtown is full of surprises. Be sure to ask the locals for their favourite hidden gems!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Irishtown has managed to retain its old-world charm, it's also embracing the future with several new developments in the pipeline. It's an exciting time to be part of this vibrant community.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Irishtown Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Irishtown. It's a place where community spirit is alive and well.
