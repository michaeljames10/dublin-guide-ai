Welcome to Smithfield: The Heart of Dublin's Northside

History

Smithfield, once a bustling market area, has evolved into a vibrant, cultural hub. Its rich history includes the famous Smithfield Horse Fair, a tradition dating back to the 17th century.

Attractions

From the iconic Jameson Distillery to the Light House Cinema, Smithfield is packed with attractions. Don't miss the panoramic views from the top of the Smithfield Tower!

Restaurants & Pubs

Smithfield is a foodie's paradise. Try traditional Irish fare at The Cobblestone Pub or enjoy a modern twist at Third Space. For drinks, Generator Hostel's bar is a local favourite.

Green Spaces

Smithfield Square is a lovely spot for a picnic. For a nature walk, the nearby Phoenix Park is a must-visit.

Local Life

Smithfield has a friendly, community vibe. It's a mix of old Dublin charm and modern city living, making it a unique place to call home.

Schools

Smithfield boasts several excellent primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded St. Paul's Primary School.

House Prices

House prices in Smithfield are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a mix of modern apartments and charming townhouses.

Commuting & Transport

Smithfield is well-served by Luas, buses, and cycleways. It's also highly walkable, with the city centre just a short stroll away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Smithfield is full of surprises. Look out for the 'Smithfield Stoneybatter Food Festival' and the legendary 'Bram Stoker Festival'.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Smithfield is an up-and-coming area, with several new developments planned. Yet, it retains its unique charm and character.

Local Events & Markets

Smithfield hosts regular community events, including the popular Smithfield Market Fair and the annual St. Patrick's Festival.
