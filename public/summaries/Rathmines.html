Welcome to Rathmines: Your Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Suburb

History

Once a quiet village outside Dublin, Rathmines has evolved into a bustling urban hub. It's rich in history, from the 14th-century Rathmines Castle to the iconic Stella Cinema, which has been entertaining locals since 1923.

Attractions

There's never a dull moment in Rathmines. Visit the Swan Shopping Centre, catch a film at the Stella Cinema, or explore the Rathmines Library. Don't miss the beautiful Rathmines Church with its distinctive dome.

Restaurants & Pubs

From cosy pubs like Mother Reilly's to trendy eateries like Farmer Browns, Rathmines has a vibrant food and drink scene. Locals recommend Jo'Burger and Manifesto for a bite to eat.

Green Spaces

Despite its urban vibe, Rathmines is home to several green spaces. Enjoy a stroll in Palmerston Park or along the Grand Canal, or have a picnic in Belgrave Square.

Local Life

Rathmines is a lively, diverse neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It's a mix of students, young professionals, and families, all contributing to the area's vibrant personality.

Schools

Rathmines boasts several excellent schools, including Rathmines College and St. Louis High School. The area is also home to the Dublin Business School and the Rathmines College of Further Education.

House Prices

House prices in Rathmines are generally higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's popularity and amenities. Expect to pay a premium for period properties and homes close to the canal.

Rent Prices

Rent in Rathmines can be pricey, with 1-bed apartments averaging €1,500, 2-bed apartments €2,000, and 3-bed houses €2,500. Compared to the Dublin average, Rathmines is less affordable. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Rathmines is generally safe, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Getting around is easy in Rathmines. The area is served by several bus routes, and the Luas green line is just a short walk away. Rathmines is also very walkable and bike-friendly.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bram Stoker, author of Dracula, was a Rathmines resident? Or that the area is home to the smallest pub in Dublin, The Barge? Discover these and more local secrets when you explore Rathmines.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Rathmines is a sought-after area with a strong property market. While it retains its historic charm, new developments and refurbishments are adding a modern edge.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers market at the Swan Centre to the annual Rathmines Festival, there's always something happening in Rathmines.
