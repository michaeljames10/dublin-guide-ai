Welcome to Cherrywood: Your Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Cherrywood, once a quiet rural area, has evolved into a bustling suburban neighbourhood. Its history is rich with tales of ancient settlements, and the area is dotted with remnants of its past, including the Tully Church and Lehaunstown Park.

Attractions

From the stunning Cabinteely Park to the historic Tully Church, Cherrywood is full of interesting spots. Don't miss the Cherrywood Business Park, a modern hub of commerce and technology.

Restaurants & Pubs

Cherrywood boasts a variety of eateries and pubs. Locals recommend the hearty fare at The Cherrywood Pub and the delicious Indian cuisine at Spice of India.

Green Spaces

Cherrywood is known for its green spaces. Enjoy a leisurely stroll in Cabinteely Park or explore the nature trails in Lehaunstown Park.

Local Life

Life in Cherrywood is a blend of modern convenience and old-world charm. The community is friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of local pride.

Schools

Cherrywood is home to several excellent primary and secondary schools, including St. Kevin's National School and Cabinteely Community School.

Cherrywood is well-served by public transport, including the Luas Green Line and several bus routes. The area is also highly walkable, with plenty of cycleways and pedestrian paths.
