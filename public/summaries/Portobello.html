Welcome to Portobello: Dublin's Vibrant and Historic Neighbourhood

History

Portobello, once a small fishing village, has evolved into a bustling neighbourhood with a rich history. It was a key location during the 1916 Rising and has been home to many famous Dubliners, including George Bernard Shaw.

Attractions

From the iconic Portobello Bridge to the bustling Portobello Market, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the street art on Lennox Street or the historic Jewish Museum on Walworth Road.

Restaurants & Pubs

Portobello is a foodie's paradise. Try the local seafood at L. Mulligan Grocer or enjoy a pint at The Bernard Shaw, a local favourite. For a unique dining experience, visit Bibi's Cafe.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll along the Grand Canal or relax in the peaceful Portobello Park. The area is also home to several community gardens, perfect for a bit of urban farming.

Local Life

Portobello is a vibrant, diverse neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It's a place where you'll find artists, musicians, and families living side by side.

Schools

There are several excellent schools in the area, including St. Kevin's Primary School and Synge Street CBS. The area is also home to Griffith College, one of Ireland's leading third-level institutions.

House Prices

House prices in Portobello are generally higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's popularity and central location.

Rent Prices

Rents in Portobello can be quite high, with a 1-bed flat typically costing around €1,500 per month. However, the area's charm and convenience make it worth the price. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Portobello is generally a safe area, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Portobello is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes and the Luas Green Line nearby. It's also a great area for cycling, with plenty of bike lanes and Dublin Bikes stations.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Portobello was once home to a thriving Jewish community? Or that the area is named after a battle in the Caribbean? Discover these stories and more as you explore the neighbourhood.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Portobello is a sought-after area with a strong property market. While there are some new developments planned, the area's historic charm is set to remain intact.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly Portobello Market to the annual Canalaphonic Music Festival, there's always something happening in Portobello.
