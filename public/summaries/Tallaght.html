Welcome to Tallaght: The Heart of South Dublin

History

From its humble beginnings as a small village in the 8th century, Tallaght has grown into a bustling urban centre. It's rich in history, with landmarks like the 13th-century Tallaght Castle and St. Maelruain's Church.

Attractions

There's plenty to see and do in Tallaght. Visit the Tallaght Stadium, home to Shamrock Rovers FC, or explore the Square Shopping Centre. Don't miss the Civic Theatre for a taste of local culture.

Restaurants & Pubs

From traditional Irish pubs like the Dragon Inn to modern eateries like the Blazin' Grill, Tallaght offers a variety of dining options. Locals recommend the hearty breakfast at the Metro Café.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at Sean Walsh Park, or take a nature walk along the Dodder River. The Tymon Park is perfect for a family picnic or a game of football.

Local Life

Tallaght is a vibrant community with a friendly, down-to-earth vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several excellent schools in Tallaght, including St. Mark's Community School and Tallaght Community School. The Institute of Technology Tallaght is also located here.

House Prices

House prices in Tallaght are generally more affordable than in central Dublin. The average price for a three-bedroom house is around €300,000.

Rent Prices

Rent prices are also relatively affordable. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a one-bedroom apartment, €1,500 for a two-bedroom, and €1,800 for a three-bedroom. Compared to the Dublin average, Tallaght is quite affordable. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Tallaght is generally safe, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Getting around Tallaght is easy with the Luas red line, numerous bus routes, and cycleways. The city centre is just a 30-minute commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Discover the local legend of the Hellfire Club, a haunted hunting lodge on Montpelier Hill. Or visit the lesser-known Kilnamanagh Castle, a hidden gem in the heart of Tallaght.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Tallaght is an up-and-coming area with several new developments planned. Yet, it retains its charming local character.

Local Events & Markets

Check out the weekly farmers market at the Square, or join the annual Tallafest community festival. Sports fans will love the local GAA and football matches.
