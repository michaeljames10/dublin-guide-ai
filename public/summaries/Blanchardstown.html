Welcome to Blanchardstown: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Suburb

History

Blanchardstown, named after the Blanchard family who gained possession of it in 1258, has evolved from a small village to one of Ireland's largest suburbs. It's rich in history, with landmarks like the 15th-century Coolmine Castle and the 19th-century St. Brigid's Church.

Restaurants & Pubs

Blanchardstown boasts a variety of eateries and pubs. Try some traditional Irish fare at The Bell Pub or enjoy a pint at The Greyhound Inn, a local favourite.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at Millennium Park, with its beautiful lake and walking trails. Or visit the St. Catherine's Park, a local gem with playgrounds and sports facilities.

Local Life

Blanchardstown is a vibrant, diverse community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. It's a place where you'll find a mix of cultures, ages, and lifestyles, all contributing to the area's unique character.

Schools

Blanchardstown is home to several primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded Castleknock Community College and the Institute of Technology Blanchardstown.

Blanchardstown is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes and nearby train stations. It's also a great area for cycling, with plenty of bike lanes and paths.
