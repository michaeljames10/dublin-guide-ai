Welcome to Blanchardstown: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Suburb

History

Blanchardstown, named after the Blanchard family who gained possession of it in 1258, has evolved from a small village to one of Ireland's largest suburbs. It's rich in history, with landmarks like the 15th-century Coolmine Castle and the 19th-century St. Brigid's Church.

Attractions

From the bustling Blanchardstown Centre, one of Ireland's largest shopping centres, to the Draíocht Arts Centre, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the National Aquatic Centre, one of the world's largest indoor water centres.

Restaurants & Pubs

Blanchardstown boasts a variety of eateries and pubs. Try some traditional Irish fare at The Bell Pub or enjoy a pint at The Greyhound Inn, a local favourite.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at Millennium Park, with its beautiful lake and walking trails. Or visit the St. Catherine's Park, a local gem with playgrounds and sports facilities.

Local Life

Blanchardstown is a vibrant, diverse community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. It's a place where you'll find a mix of cultures, ages, and lifestyles, all contributing to the area's unique character.

Schools

Blanchardstown is home to several primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded Castleknock Community College and the Institute of Technology Blanchardstown.

House Prices

House prices in Blanchardstown are generally more affordable than in central Dublin, with the average price for a three-bedroom house around €300,000.

Rent Prices

Rents are also more affordable, with a one-bedroom apartment typically costing around €1,200 per month, a two-bedroom around €1,500, and a three-bedroom around €1,800. Compared to the Dublin average, Blanchardstown is quite affordable. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Blanchardstown is generally safe, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Blanchardstown is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes and nearby train stations. It's also a great area for cycling, with plenty of bike lanes and paths.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Discover the secret garden at Farmleigh House, or learn about the local legend of the haunted Ongar House. And don't forget to try a "Blanchardstown Bap" at the local bakery!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Blanchardstown is a growing area with several new developments planned, including a new town centre and residential areas. It's an exciting time to be part of this vibrant community.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Blanchardstown Christmas Market to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening. And don't miss the local GAA matches, a true Irish tradition.
