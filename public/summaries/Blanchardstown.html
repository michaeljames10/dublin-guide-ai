Welcome to Blanchardstown: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Suburb

History

Blanchardstown, named after the Blanchard family who gained possession of it in 1258, has evolved from a small village to one of Ireland's largest suburbs. It's rich in history, with landmarks like the 15th-century Coolmine Castle and the Royal Canal, which played a key role in the area's development.

Attractions

From the bustling Blanchardstown Centre, one of Ireland's largest shopping centres, to the Draíocht Arts Centre and the National Aquatic Centre, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the historic Farmleigh House and the beautiful Phoenix Park nearby.

Restaurants & Pubs

Blanchardstown boasts a variety of eateries and pubs. Locals recommend The Bell Pub for a pint, and for food, try Maximilian's Bistro or the Gourmet Food Parlour. For a taste of Asia, visit Camile Thai.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at Millennium Park, with its playgrounds and skate park, or take a stroll along the Royal Canal. The nearby Phoenix Park is a haven for nature lovers and fitness enthusiasts.

Local Life

Blanchardstown has a vibrant, friendly community vibe. It's a diverse area with a mix of families, young professionals, and students, thanks to its proximity to the Institute of Technology Blanchardstown.

Schools

The area is home to several primary and secondary schools, including Scoil Bhríde and Castleknock Community College. The Institute of Technology Blanchardstown is a notable higher education institution.

House Prices

House prices in Blanchardstown are generally lower than the Dublin average, with a mix of apartments, terraced houses, and semi-detached homes available.

Rent Prices

Rents are more affordable compared to the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,300 for a 1-bed, €1,600 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed place. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Blanchardstown is generally safe, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Blanchardstown is well-served by buses, and the nearby Castleknock train station provides easy access to the city centre. The area is also bike-friendly, with several cycleways.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Check out the secret garden at Farmleigh House, and don't miss the annual Rose Festival in nearby St. Anne's Park. Famous past residents include musician Phil Lynott and actor Colin Farrell.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Blanchardstown is a growing area with several new developments planned, including residential, commercial, and public spaces.

Local Events & Markets

Enjoy the weekly farmers market at Tyrrelstown Park, the annual Halloween Spooktacular, and the Christmas lights switch-on at Blanchardstown Centre.
