Welcome to Lucan, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Lucan's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From its origins as a Viking settlement to its growth during the Georgian era, Lucan has always been a place of interest. The area is home to Lucan House, one of Ireland's most significant stately homes, and the charming old village centre still retains its historic character.

Attractions

Lucan is a treasure trove of attractions. From the stunning Lucan Demesne and Liffey Valley, to the historic Lucan House and Spa, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the chance to take a stroll along the River Liffey, or visit the local golf clubs for a round or two.

Restaurants & Pubs

Lucan's food and drink scene is as vibrant as a Dublin pub on a Friday night. From traditional Irish pubs like The Penny Hill or Kenny's, to delicious eateries like Carrolls Gastro Pub or Courtney's Steakhouse, there's something for every palate.

Green Spaces

Lucan is a green oasis on Dublin's doorstep. The area boasts several parks and nature walks, including St. Catherine's Park and Lucan Demesne. Whether you're a keen walker, a bird watcher, or just enjoy a picnic in the park, Lucan has you covered.

Local Life

Life in Lucan is a blend of traditional Irish charm and modern convenience. The community is friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of local pride. The area has a relaxed, village-like atmosphere, but with the buzz of Dublin city just a short commute away.

Schools

Lucan is home to a number of excellent primary and secondary schools, including Lucan Community College and St. Joseph's College. The area also boasts several notable institutions, such as the Institute of Technology, Blanchardstown.

House Prices

House prices in Lucan are generally more affordable than in central Dublin. The area offers a mix of property types, from charming period homes to modern apartments and family houses.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Lucan are typically lower than the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Lucan is generally a safe area, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Lucan is well-served by public transport, with regular bus services to Dublin city centre. The area is also close to the M50 motorway, making it easy to get around by car. For cyclists, there are several cycle paths and greenways to explore.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Lucan is full of hidden gems and local legends. From the haunted Lucan House to the secret tunnels of the old spa, there's always something new to discover. And don't forget to keep an eye out for the famous Lucan Pigeons!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Lucan is an area on the rise, with several new developments planned for the coming years. However, the area retains its unique charm and character, making it a great place to live or invest.

Local Events & Markets

Lucan is a hub of community activity, with regular events and markets. From the annual Lucan Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something going on.
