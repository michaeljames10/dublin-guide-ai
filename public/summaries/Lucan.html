Welcome to Lucan, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Lucan's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From its origins as a Viking settlement to its growth during the Georgian era, Lucan has always been a place of importance. The area is steeped in heritage, with many historical buildings and landmarks still standing today.

Attractions

Lucan is home to a number of attractions, including the stunning Lucan House and Demesne, the charming village centre, and the bustling Liffey Valley Shopping Centre. There's always something to see and do in Lucan!

Restaurants & Pubs

From traditional Irish pubs to modern eateries, Lucan has a vibrant food and drink scene. Locals recommend The Penny Hill for a hearty meal and a pint, or Courtyard Restaurant for a more refined dining experience.

Green Spaces

Lucan is blessed with beautiful green spaces. St. Catherine's Park is a local favourite for a leisurely stroll or a family picnic. The Lucan Demesne is another great spot for nature lovers.

Local Life

Life in Lucan is a blend of traditional charm and modern convenience. The community is friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of local pride. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses are supported.

Schools

Lucan boasts a number of excellent primary and secondary schools. Notable institutions include Lucan Community College and St. Joseph's College.

House Prices

House prices in Lucan are generally more affordable than in central Dublin. The area offers a mix of property types, from charming period homes to modern apartments.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Lucan are typically lower than the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Lucan is generally a safe area, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Lucan is well-served by public transport, with regular bus services to Dublin city centre. The area is also popular with cyclists, thanks to its network of cycle paths.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Lucan is full of hidden gems and local legends. Don't miss the chance to visit the old spa, a historic site with a fascinating story. And keep an eye out for the ghost of Lucan House, said to haunt the grounds!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Lucan is a growing area, with new developments planned for the future. But despite its growth, the area retains its unique charm and community spirit.

Local Events & Markets

Lucan is a hub of community activity, with regular events and markets. The annual Lucan Festival is a highlight, featuring live music, food stalls, and family-friendly activities.

Area Vibe Score
