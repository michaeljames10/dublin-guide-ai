Welcome to Ballymun: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Northside Gem

History

Once a rural area, Ballymun underwent a massive transformation in the 1960s with the construction of Ireland's first high-rise flats. Despite facing challenges, the area has shown resilience and is now a vibrant community with a rich cultural heritage.

Attractions

From the Axis Ballymun arts centre to the Ballymun Market, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the Ballymun Tower, the last remaining symbol of the old Ballymun.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, locals recommend The Comet. And for a pint, you can't beat The Towers Pub.

Green Spaces

Albert College Park and Poppintree Park are perfect for a stroll or a picnic. And for the more adventurous, there's the Ballymun Adventure Sports Hub.

Local Life

Ballymun is a close-knit community with a strong sense of identity. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in the area, including Ballymun Comprehensive, one of the most progressive schools in the country.

House Prices

House prices in Ballymun are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it an affordable option for first-time buyers.

Rent Prices

Rents are also lower than average. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

While Ballymun has faced challenges, it's a community that looks out for each other. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With several bus routes and close proximity to the M50, getting around is easy. And the city centre is just a short bus ride away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that U2 played their first gig in Ballymun? And don't miss the annual Ballymun Festival, a celebration of local culture and community.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With ongoing regeneration and a strong community spirit, Ballymun is an area on the up.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly Ballymun Market to the annual Ballymun Festival, there's always something happening.
