Welcome to Ballymun: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Northside Gem

History

Once a rural area, Ballymun underwent a massive transformation in the 1960s with the construction of Ireland's first high-rise flats. Today, it's a vibrant community with a rich history and a strong sense of local pride.

Attractions

Don't miss the Ballymun Tower, the last remaining high-rise, now a beacon of the area's past. The Axis Ballymun is a cultural hub, hosting theatre, music, and art events.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a hearty meal, locals recommend The Comet. For a pint, you can't beat The Towers Pub, a true Ballymun institution.

Green Spaces

Albert College Park is a local favourite for a stroll or a picnic. The Poppintree Park is perfect for a family day out with its playground and sports facilities.

Local Life

Ballymun is a close-knit community with a strong sense of identity. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses are supported.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in Ballymun, including the notable Trinity Comprehensive School.

House Prices

House prices in Ballymun are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it an affordable option for first-time buyers.
