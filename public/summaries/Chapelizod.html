Welcome to Chapelizod: Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Chapelizod, a charming village nestled by the River Liffey, has a rich history dating back to the 12th century. It's named after the chapel of Isolde, linked to the famous Tristan and Isolde legend. Over the centuries, it's evolved from a rural hamlet to a bustling part of Dublin, while retaining its unique character.

Attractions

From the historic Phoenix Park to the quaint Chapelizod Village Centre, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the charming St. Laurence's Church and the Phoenix Park Visitor Centre.

Green Spaces

Chapelizod is a green oasis, with Phoenix Park - Europe's largest city park - on its doorstep. Enjoy nature walks, cycling, or a picnic amidst deer in the park.

Local Life

Life in Chapelizod is a blend of village charm and city convenience. The community is tight-knit, friendly, and proud of their local heritage.

Schools

Chapelizod boasts several good schools, including Chapelizod National School and St. Patrick's National School. It's also close to several secondary schools and colleges in Dublin.

House Prices

House prices in Chapelizod are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.
