Welcome to Chapelizod: Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Chapelizod, a charming village nestled by the River Liffey, has a rich history dating back to the 12th century. It's named after the chapel of Isolde, linked to the famous Tristan and Isolde legend. Over the centuries, it's evolved from a rural hamlet to a bustling part of Dublin, while retaining its unique character.

Attractions

From the historic Phoenix Park to the quaint village centre, Chapelizod is brimming with attractions. Don't miss the stunning St. Laurence's Church and the charming local shops.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a pint and a chat, head to The Villager Pub. If you're peckish, try the local favourite, The Chapelizod Bistro, known for its hearty Irish fare.

Green Spaces

Chapelizod is a green oasis, with Phoenix Park on its doorstep. Enjoy a stroll along the Liffey, or explore the park's trails and gardens.

Local Life

Life in Chapelizod is a blend of village charm and city convenience. The community is tight-knit and friendly, with a lively local scene.

Schools

Chapelizod boasts several excellent schools, including Chapelizod National School and St. Patrick's National School. It's also close to several top secondary schools.

House Prices

House prices in Chapelizod are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.
