Welcome to Balbriggan: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Founded by the Vikings in the 11th century, Balbriggan has a rich history. It was once a bustling fishing port and a major player in the hosiery and cotton industry. Today, it's a vibrant, multicultural town with a strong sense of community.

Attractions

From the historic Balbriggan Lighthouse to the beautiful Ardgillan Castle, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the Martello Tower and the stunning coastal walks.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, try the Brick Room for delicious brunch or Libero's for authentic Italian. For a pint, you can't beat the atmosphere in O'Dwyers or the Harbour Bar.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the great outdoors at Ardgillan Park, or take a stroll along the beach. The town is also home to several well-maintained golf courses.

Local Life

Life in Balbriggan is laid-back and friendly. The town has a strong community spirit, with plenty of local events and sports clubs. It's a great place to raise a family, with a safe, welcoming vibe.

Schools

There are several excellent primary and secondary schools in the area, including Balbriggan Educate Together and Loreto Secondary School. The town is also home to a campus of the Dublin and Dun Laoghaire Education and Training Board.

Commuting & Transport

With a train station in the town centre and several bus routes, commuting to Dublin city centre is easy. The town is also well-served by cycle paths and is very walkable.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Check out the secret garden at Ardgillan Castle, or try to spot the ghost of the White Lady who is said to haunt the castle grounds. And don't forget to look out for the annual Balbriggan Fish & Farmers Market.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new housing developments and improvements to local amenities, Balbriggan is an up-and-coming area with a bright future.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Balbriggan Summerfest to the weekly farmers market, there's always something happening in town.
