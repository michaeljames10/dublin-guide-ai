Welcome to Balbriggan: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Founded by the Vikings in the 11th century, Balbriggan has a rich history. It was once a bustling fishing port and a major player in the hosiery and cotton industry. Today, it's a vibrant, multicultural town with a strong sense of community.

Attractions

From the historic Balbriggan Lighthouse to the beautiful Ardgillan Castle, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the Martello Tower and the stunning coastal walks.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, try the Brick Room or Libero's. For a pint, you can't beat the atmosphere in O'Dwyer's or the Milestone Inn.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the great outdoors at Ardgillan Park, or take a stroll along the beach. The town is also home to several well-maintained golf courses.

Local Life

Life in Balbriggan is laid-back and friendly. The town has a diverse population and a strong sense of community spirit. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several excellent primary and secondary schools in the area, including Balbriggan Educate Together and Loreto Secondary School.

House Prices

House prices in Balbriggan are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.

Rent Prices

Rent prices are also more affordable than in many other parts of Dublin. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,400 for a 2-bed, and €1,600 for a 3-bed. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

With a low crime index, Balbriggan is a safe place to live. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular trains to Dublin city centre, commuting is a breeze. The town is also well-served by buses, and there are plenty of cycle paths and walkways.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Balbriggan was once visited by King George IV? Or that it's home to the world's only public sundial? Discover these and more local legends when you visit.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new developments planned and a growing population, Balbriggan is a town on the rise. But despite its growth, it retains its small-town charm and community spirit.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Balbriggan Summerfest to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in town.

Area Vibe Score
