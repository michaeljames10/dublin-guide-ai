Welcome to Sallynoggin: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a small rural village, Sallynoggin has grown into a vibrant suburb of Dublin. Its history is steeped in the rich tapestry of Ireland's past, with landmarks dating back to the 19th century.

Attractions

From the historic Sallynoggin Church to the bustling Sallynoggin Market, there's plenty to see and do in this charming neighbourhood.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew at The Sallynoggin Inn or a pint of Guinness at O'Donoghue's, Sallynoggin's food and drink scene won't disappoint.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a leisurely stroll in Pearse Park or take in the stunning views from Killiney Hill. Sallynoggin is a green oasis in the heart of Dublin.

Local Life

Sallynoggin is a close-knit community with a laid-back vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name and there's always a friendly face to greet you.

Schools

With a number of highly-rated primary and secondary schools, Sallynoggin is a great place for families. The area is also home to the renowned Sallynoggin College of Further Education.

House Prices

House prices in Sallynoggin are generally more affordable than in central Dublin, with a mix of traditional and modern homes on offer.

With regular bus services and close proximity to the DART, getting around is a breeze. Plus, Sallynoggin is just a short drive from Dublin city centre.
