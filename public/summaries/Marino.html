Welcome to Marino, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Marino, a small, intimate neighbourhood, has a rich history dating back to the 1920s. It's known for its unique 'garden city' architecture and the iconic Marino Casino, a stunning 18th-century neoclassical building.

Attractions

From the Marino Casino to the beautiful St. Vincent's Park, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the local GAA club, a hub of community activity and a great place to catch a hurling match.

Restaurants & Pubs

Marino might be small, but it's got some cracking spots for a bite or a pint. Locals love the cosy atmosphere at The Casino at Marino and the hearty fare at Gourmet Food Parlour.

Green Spaces

Marino is blessed with green spaces. St. Vincent's Park is a local favourite, perfect for a leisurely stroll or a family picnic. The area is also home to several community gardens.

Local Life

Marino is a tight-knit community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. It's a quiet, residential area with a strong sense of local pride and a slower pace of life.

Schools

Marino has several excellent schools, including Marino College and St. Vincent's Primary School. The area is also home to Marino Institute of Education, a well-regarded teacher training college.

House Prices

House prices in Marino are generally on par with the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €400,000 for a three-bedroom home.

Rent Prices

Rents in Marino are slightly below the Dublin average. A one-bedroom flat typically costs around €1,200 per month, while a three-bedroom house goes for about €2,000. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Marino is a safe, family-friendly area with a low crime rate. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Marino is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes passing through the area. It's also a short walk to the Clontarf Road DART station, and the city centre is just a 15-minute cycle away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Marino is full of surprises. Look out for the Fairy Tree in St. Vincent's Park, a local landmark adorned with children's wishes. And don't forget to visit the Marino Casino, a hidden architectural gem.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Marino is a stable, established neighbourhood that's managed to retain its unique character and charm. There are no major developments planned, so it's likely to stay that way for the foreseeable future.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Marino Fun Day to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Marino. The local GAA club also hosts regular matches and community events.
