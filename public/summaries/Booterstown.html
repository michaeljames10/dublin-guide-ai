Welcome to Booterstown: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Booterstown, or Baile an Bhóthair in Irish, has a rich history dating back to the 13th century. It was once a small fishing village and has evolved into a charming residential area with a unique blend of old-world charm and modern amenities.

Attractions

Booterstown is home to the Booterstown Bird Sanctuary, a must-visit for nature lovers. Don't miss the historic Booterstown Church, a beautiful example of Georgian architecture.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, locals recommend Gleesons of Booterstown, a cosy gastropub. For a pint, you can't beat the atmosphere at The Old Punch Bowl.

Green Spaces

Booterstown is blessed with plenty of green spaces. Booterstown Park is a local favourite for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.

Local Life

Living in Booterstown is like being part of a close-knit community. It's a quiet, friendly neighbourhood with a relaxed vibe.

Schools

Booterstown has several excellent primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded St. Andrew's College.

House Prices

House prices in Booterstown tend to be above the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and excellent amenities.

Rent Prices

Rents for a 1-bed, 2-bed, and 3-bed places are typically higher than the Dublin average. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Commuting & Transport

Booterstown is well-served by public transport, including the DART and several bus routes. It's also a great area for cycling and walking.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

One of Booterstown's best-kept secrets is the annual Swan Festival, a quirky local tradition that celebrates the area's feathered residents.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Booterstown is a stable neighbourhood that's retaining its charm while embracing modern amenities.

Local Events & Markets

Booterstown hosts a popular farmers' market every weekend, and the annual Booterstown Festival is a highlight of the local calendar.
