Welcome to Booterstown: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Booterstown, or Baile an Bhóthair in Irish, has a rich history dating back to the 13th century. It was once a small fishing village and has evolved into a charming residential area, retaining its historical charm with landmarks like the Booterstown Church and Marsh.

Attractions

Booterstown is home to the Booterstown Nature Reserve, a haven for bird watchers. The Booterstown Dart Station, a beautiful piece of Victorian architecture, is another must-see.

Restaurants & Pubs

From the cosy O'Neill's Pub to the delicious seafood at Lobstar, Booterstown offers a variety of dining options that locals swear by.

Green Spaces

Booterstown is blessed with green spaces. The Booterstown Nature Reserve is a local favourite, offering a peaceful retreat from city life.

Local Life

Life in Booterstown is a blend of tranquillity and convenience. The community is tight-knit, and the area exudes a friendly, village-like vibe.

Schools

Booterstown is home to several reputable schools, including Booterstown National School and St. Andrew's College.

House Prices

House prices in Booterstown tend to be above the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and excellent amenities.

Rent Prices

Crime Index

Commuting & Transport

Booterstown is well-served by public transport, with the Dart and several bus routes providing easy access to the city centre. The area is also very walkable and cyclist-friendly.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Booterstown is full of surprises, from the hidden bird sanctuary in the marsh to the legendary ghost of the Old Punch Bowl pub.

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Booterstown has largely retained its traditional charm, there are exciting new developments on the horizon, including a planned upgrade of the Dart station.

Local Events & Markets

Booterstown hosts a variety of community events, from the annual Christmas fair to the popular farmers' market at the Dart station.
