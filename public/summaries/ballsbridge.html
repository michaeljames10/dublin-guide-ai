Welcome to Ballsbridge: Dublin's Charming and Vibrant Neighbourhood

History

Originally known as 'Pembroke Township', Ballsbridge is steeped in history. It's named after the 17th-century bridge built by a certain Mr. Ball. The area has evolved from a rural landscape into one of Dublin's most prestigious residential and business districts.

Attractions

From the iconic Aviva Stadium to the Royal Dublin Society, Ballsbridge is brimming with attractions. Don't miss the stunning Herbert Park and the Embassy Row, home to many foreign embassies.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of Guinness, Ballsbridge has you covered. Locals recommend The Bridge 1859 for a cosy pub experience and Roly's Bistro for a fine dining treat.

Green Spaces

Herbert Park is the jewel in Ballsbridge's crown, offering a serene escape from city life. It's perfect for a leisurely stroll, a picnic, or even a game of tennis.

Local Life

Ballsbridge is a vibrant, friendly neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It's a place where people know their neighbours and local businesses thrive.

Schools

Home to some of Dublin's top schools, including St. Conleth's College and The Teresian School, Ballsbridge is a great place for families.

House Prices

As one of Dublin's most sought-after areas, house prices in Ballsbridge are on the higher end. Expect to pay a premium for the privilege of living here.

Commuting & Transport

With excellent Luas, Dart, and bus connections, getting around is a breeze. The area is also highly walkable and boasts several cycleways.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bram Stoker, author of Dracula, was born in Clontarf, just a stone's throw from Ballsbridge? And don't miss the secret garden in Herbert Park!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Ballsbridge retains its historic charm, it's also embracing the future with exciting new developments like the Lansdowne Place luxury apartments.
