Welcome to Cabra, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a rural village, Cabra has grown into a bustling urban neighbourhood with a rich history. From its origins in the 19th century to its development as a residential area in the 20th century, Cabra has always been a place of community and culture.

Attractions

From the historic Phoenix Park to the modern Cabra Library, there's plenty to see and do in Cabra. Don't miss the local street art and the charming old-world architecture.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of the black stuff, Cabra's got you covered. Locals recommend The Circular for a cosy pub experience and Ravi's Kitchen for top-notch Indian cuisine.

Green Spaces

With Phoenix Park on its doorstep and several local parks, Cabra is a green oasis in the city. Perfect for a leisurely stroll or a family picnic.

Local Life

Cabra is a vibrant, friendly neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

Home to several primary and secondary schools, Cabra is a great place for families. The area is also close to Dublin City University and the National College of Art and Design.

House Prices

House prices in Cabra are generally more affordable than in other parts of Dublin, with a mix of older terraced houses and newer developments.

Rent Prices

Rent in Cabra is generally more affordable than the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Cabra is generally a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With several bus routes, a nearby Luas stop, and plenty of cycle lanes, getting around Cabra is a breeze. The city centre is just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the legendary 'Cabra Cat' to the secret garden at the end of Carnlough Road, Cabra is full of surprises. And don't forget to check out the local street art!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new developments and a growing popularity, Cabra is definitely an up-and-coming area. But don't worry, it's still got plenty of that old-world charm.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers' market to the annual Cabra Festival, there's always something happening in Cabra. And don't miss the local GAA matches!
