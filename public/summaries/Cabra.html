Welcome to Cabra, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Originally a rural village, Cabra has grown into a bustling urban neighbourhood with a rich history. From its roots as a dairy farming community to its role in the 1916 Rising, Cabra's past is as diverse as its residents.

Attractions

From the iconic Cabra Castle to the quirky Cabra Park, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the local street art and the historic Phoenix Park nearby.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of the black stuff, Cabra's got you covered. Locals recommend The Washerwoman for food and The Cobblestone for traditional music.

Green Spaces

With Phoenix Park on its doorstep and several local parks, Cabra is a green oasis in the city. Perfect for a Sunday stroll or a picnic with the family.

Local Life

Cabra is a vibrant, friendly neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

Home to several primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded St. Declan's College, Cabra is a great place for families.

House Prices

While prices have risen in recent years, Cabra still offers good value compared to other Dublin neighbourhoods. Expect to pay around €350,000 for a three-bedroom house.

Rent Prices

Rents are relatively affordable, with a one-bed costing around €1,200, a two-bed €1,500, and a three-bed €1,800. That's a solid 7/10 on the affordability scale. 💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Cabra is generally safe, with a crime index rating of 7/10. 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular buses, nearby Luas and Dart stations, and plenty of cycle lanes, getting around is a breeze. Plus, it's just a 15-minute drive to the city centre.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the legendary Cabra Cat to the secret tunnel under the railway, Cabra is full of surprises. And don't forget to check out the local farmers' market!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new developments and a growing popularity, Cabra is definitely on the up. But don't worry, it's still got that old-school Dublin charm.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Cabra Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening. And don't miss the local GAA matches!

Area Vibe Score

Overall Vibe: A friendly, community-focused neighbourhood with a touch of urban cool.
