Welcome to Portmarnock: The Gem of Dublin's North Coast

The area evolved from a small fishing village to a bustling seaside town, thanks to the arrival of the railway in the 19th century. The famous Portmarnock Golf Club, founded in 1894, has hosted many international tournaments and is a testament to the area's sporting heritage.

Attractions

From the stunning Portmarnock Beach to the historic Martello Tower, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the Velvet Strand, a 5km stretch of sandy beach with breathtaking views of the Dublin Mountains.

Restaurants & Pubs

Portmarnock offers a variety of dining options, from the award-winning White Sands Hotel to the cosy local pub, Gibney's. For seafood lovers, the Octopussy's Seafood Tapas bar is a must-visit.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a leisurely stroll in the Portmarnock Sports and Leisure Club park, or take a nature walk along the coastal path. The area is also home to several golf courses, including the world-renowned Portmarnock Golf Club.

Local Life

Life in Portmarnock is laid-back and friendly, with a strong sense of community. The area is known for its sporting spirit, with local football, GAA, and golf clubs playing a big role in community life.

Schools

Portmarnock has several well-regarded primary and secondary schools, including St. Marnock's National School and Portmarnock Community School. The area is also close to Dublin City University and the Institute of Technology, Blanchardstown.

Commuting & Transport

Portmarnock is well-served by public transport, with regular DART and bus services to Dublin city centre. The area is also popular with cyclists and walkers, thanks to its scenic coastal paths.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Portmarnock was the starting point for the first successful flight across the Atlantic? Or that the area is home to a haunted 19th-century mansion? Discover these stories and more as you explore Portmarnock's hidden gems.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its mix of historic charm and modern amenities, Portmarnock continues to attract homebuyers and investors. Future developments include new residential and commercial projects, as well as improvements to local infrastructure.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Portmarnock Summer Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Portmarnock. The area is also known for its vibrant sports scene, with regular matches and tournaments.
