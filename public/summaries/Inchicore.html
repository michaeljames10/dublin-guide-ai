Welcome to Inchicore, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a rural village, Inchicore has evolved into a bustling urban neighbourhood with a rich history. It's home to the Royal Canal and the Irish railway works, which played a key role in Ireland's industrial revolution.

Attractions

From the Kilmainham Gaol Museum to the Irish Museum of Modern Art, Inchicore is a cultural hotspot. Don't miss the Phoenix Park, one of the largest city parks in Europe.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew at The Black Lion or a pint at The Patriot's Inn, Inchicore's got you covered. Locals recommend the fish and chips at The Village Inn.

Green Spaces

With the Grand Canal Walk and Phoenix Park nearby, Inchicore is a haven for nature lovers. The area is also home to several community gardens.

Local Life

Inchicore is a vibrant, close-knit community with a strong sense of identity. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

Inchicore has a range of primary and secondary schools, including the well-regarded Inchicore National School and St. Michael's Secondary School.

House Prices

House prices in Inchicore are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.

Rent Prices

Rents are also more affordable than the city average. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Inchicore is generally safe, with a crime index lower than the city average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With the Luas Red Line, several bus routes, and cycleways, getting around Inchicore is a breeze. The city centre is just a 15-minute commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Inchicore was the birthplace of famous playwright Sean O'Casey? And don't miss the secret garden at the back of the Oblates Church.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new developments and regeneration projects underway, Inchicore is an up-and-coming neighbourhood that's retaining its unique charm.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Inchicore Village Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Inchicore.
