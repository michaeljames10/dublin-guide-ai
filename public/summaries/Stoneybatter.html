Welcome to Stoneybatter: Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Stoneybatter, known in Irish as Bóthar na gCloch, has a rich history dating back to the Viking era. It's evolved from a small village to a bustling urban neighbourhood, all while retaining its unique charm and heritage.

Attractions

From the iconic Arbour Hill Cemetery to the quirky Lilliput Press, Stoneybatter is brimming with local landmarks and interesting spots. Don't miss the chance to explore the Dublin Food Co-op and the Phoenix Park nearby.

Restaurants & Pubs

Stoneybatter is a foodie's paradise. Check out the famous L. Mulligan Grocer for a hearty meal, or sip on a pint at Walsh's Pub. Locals also recommend Slice for a delightful brunch.

Green Spaces

Despite its urban setting, Stoneybatter is home to several green spaces. The Phoenix Park, Europe's largest city park, is just a stone's throw away. The Royal Canal Way is perfect for a leisurely stroll or bike ride.

Local Life

Living in Stoneybatter is like being part of a tight-knit community. The area has a vibrant, creative vibe, with a mix of young professionals, families, and artists calling it home.

Schools

Stoneybatter boasts several reputable primary and secondary schools, including the Educate Together National School and the Dublin 7 Educate Together Secondary School.

House Prices

House prices in Stoneybatter have been on the rise, with the average price for a home now around €400,000. It's a popular area for first-time buyers and young families.

Rent Prices

Rent in Stoneybatter is slightly above the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,500 for a 1-bed, €1,800 for a 2-bed, and €2,200 for a 3-bed place. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Stoneybatter is generally safe, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Stoneybatter is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes and the Luas light rail nearby. It's also a very walkable area, with the city centre just a 20-minute stroll away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Stoneybatter is full of surprises. Look out for the 'Stoneybatter Stones', a local art installation, and don't miss the annual Stoneybatter Festival, a celebration of all things local.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Stoneybatter is an up-and-coming area, with several new developments planned. However, it's also committed to preserving its unique character and charm.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers' market to the annual Stoneybatter Festival, there's always something happening in this vibrant neighbourhood.
