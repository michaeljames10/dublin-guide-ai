Welcome to Ringsend, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a bustling port town, Ringsend has evolved into a charming residential area with a rich history. From its origins as a Viking settlement to its role in the 1916 Rising, Ringsend's past is as colourful as its present.

Attractions

From the iconic Poolbeg Lighthouse to the bustling Irishtown Nature Park, Ringsend is packed with local landmarks and interesting spots to explore.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty meal at The Oarsman or a pint at The Bath Pub, Ringsend offers a variety of local eateries and watering holes that locals swear by.

Green Spaces

With the River Dodder on one side and the Grand Canal on the other, Ringsend is a haven for nature lovers. Don't miss a stroll through Ringsend Park!

Local Life

Ringsend is a tight-knit community with a laid-back vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name and local traditions are cherished.

Schools

Home to several primary and secondary schools, including the notable St. Patrick's Boys' National School, Ringsend offers quality education for its residents.
