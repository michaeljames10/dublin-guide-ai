Welcome to Foxrock, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Foxrock was developed as a residential suburb in the 19th century, with its unique charm and character preserved through the years. It's a place where history meets modernity, with a rich heritage that's evident in its beautiful period homes and leafy streets.

Attractions

From the stunning Foxrock Church to the historic Leopardstown Racecourse, there's plenty to see and do in Foxrock. Don't miss a stroll down Brighton Road, known for its beautiful Victorian and Edwardian houses.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of the black stuff, Foxrock has you covered. Locals recommend The Gables for its cosy atmosphere and delicious food.

Green Spaces

Foxrock is blessed with plenty of green spaces, including the tranquil Cabinteely Park and the picturesque Foxrock Golf Club. It's a haven for outdoor enthusiasts.

Local Life

Life in Foxrock is peaceful and relaxed, with a strong sense of community. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name and children play freely in the streets.

Schools

Foxrock boasts some of the best schools in Dublin, including Foxrock School and Loreto College. It's a great place to raise a family.

House Prices

House prices in Foxrock are on the higher end of the scale, reflecting the area's desirability and high standard of living. Expect to pay a premium for the privilege of calling Foxrock home.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Foxrock are above the Dublin average, with a 1-bed apartment typically costing around €1,500 per month. However, the area's charm and amenities make it worth every penny. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Foxrock is one of the safest areas in Dublin, with a low crime index. Residents can feel secure in their homes and on the streets. Safety: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Getting around Foxrock is a breeze, with the Luas, Dart, and numerous bus routes serving the area. The city centre is just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Foxrock was once home to Samuel Beckett, the famous Irish playwright? And don't miss the secret garden at the end of Brighton Road, a true hidden gem.

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Foxrock retains its historic charm, it's also embracing the future with exciting new developments. It's an area that's constantly evolving, yet remains true to its roots.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Foxrock Village Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Foxrock. It's a place that's full of life and community spirit.
