Welcome to Foxrock, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Founded in 1859 by William and John Bentley and Edward and Anthony Fox, Foxrock has a rich history. It was designed as an affluent garden suburb, and it still retains that charm today.

Attractions

From the stunning architecture of Tullow Church to the tranquil beauty of Leopardstown Racecourse, there's plenty to see in Foxrock.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty meal at The Gables or a pint at The Foxrock Inn, you'll find plenty of local favourites here.

Green Spaces

With Cabinteely Park on its doorstep and plenty of green spaces, Foxrock is a haven for nature lovers.

Local Life

Life in Foxrock is peaceful and community-oriented, with a friendly local vibe and plenty of character.

Schools

Foxrock boasts excellent schools, including Foxrock's Loreto College, a top-rated secondary school for girls.

House Prices

As one of Dublin's most affluent suburbs, house prices in Foxrock are on the higher end of the scale.

Rent Prices

Rents in Foxrock are above the Dublin average, with a 1-bed costing around €1,500, a 2-bed €2,000, and a 3-bed €2,500. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Foxrock is a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With the Luas Green Line and several bus routes, commuting from Foxrock to the city centre is a breeze.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Foxrock was the birthplace of famous playwright Samuel Beckett? And don't miss the secret garden at the end of Brighton Road!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Foxrock retains its historic charm, there's always something new happening, from property renovations to new local businesses.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Foxrock Village Festival to the weekly food market, there's always something going on in Foxrock.
