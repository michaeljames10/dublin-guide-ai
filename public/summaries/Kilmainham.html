Welcome to Kilmainham: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Historic Heart

History

Steeped in history, Kilmainham is home to some of Dublin's most significant landmarks. From the ancient Kilmainham Gaol to the Royal Hospital, the area has evolved from a medieval suburb to a bustling modern neighbourhood.

Attractions

Don't miss the Irish Museum of Modern Art and the War Memorial Gardens. For a unique experience, take a tour of the old Kilmainham Gaol or enjoy a stroll along the Grand Canal.

Restaurants & Pubs

From the cosy atmosphere of The Patriot's Inn to the delicious food at Union8, Kilmainham offers a variety of local dining experiences. And don't forget to grab a pint at The Royal Oak!

Green Spaces

Enjoy the tranquillity of the Phoenix Park, the largest enclosed public park in any European capital. Or take a leisurely walk along the Liffey River.

Local Life

Kilmainham is a vibrant community with a friendly, laid-back vibe. It's a place where history meets modernity, and locals enjoy a high quality of life.

Schools

There are several excellent primary and secondary schools in the area, including the notable St. James's Primary School.

House Prices

House prices in Kilmainham are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a mix of traditional and modern homes available.

Commuting & Transport

With excellent Luas and bus connections, as well as cycleways and walkable streets, getting around Kilmainham is a breeze. The city centre is just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Discover the secret garden at the IMMA, or learn about the local legend of the Black Dog Jail, a former prison said to be haunted.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With ongoing regeneration and a growing popularity, Kilmainham is an up-and-coming neighbourhood that retains its historic charm.

Local Events & Markets

Enjoy the weekly farmers' market at the Royal Hospital, or join in the annual Kilmainham Arts Festival for a taste of local culture.
