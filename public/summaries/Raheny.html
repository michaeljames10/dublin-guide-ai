Welcome to Raheny: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

From its humble beginnings as a small fishing village, Raheny has evolved into a vibrant suburb with a rich history. The area is home to the ancient St. Assam's Church, and the Raheny Heritage Society keeps the local history alive with regular events and exhibitions.

Attractions

Don't miss a visit to the beautiful St. Anne's Park, the second largest municipal park in Dublin. The park hosts the weekly Raheny Farmers Market, a local favourite. For history buffs, the Raheny Heritage Trail is a must-do.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a pint and a chat, locals recommend The Cedar Lounge. If you're peckish, head to McHugh's Wine & Dine Raheny for some top-notch grub. And for the best fish and chips in Dublin, look no further than the local institution that is Beshoff Bros.

Green Spaces

With St. Anne's Park, Bull Island, and the North Bull Island Nature Reserve, Raheny is a haven for nature lovers. There are plenty of walking trails, picnic spots, and even a local rose garden to enjoy.

Local Life

Raheny is a friendly, community-oriented area with a relaxed vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name and there's always a local event to look forward to.

Schools

Raheny boasts a number of excellent primary and secondary schools, including Raheny Primary School and St. Paul's College. The area is also home to the renowned Dublin City University.

House Prices

House prices in Raheny are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a typical 3-bed semi-detached house going for around €400,000.

Rent Prices

Rents in Raheny are slightly below the Dublin average, with a 1-bed apartment typically costing around €1,200 per month, a 2-bed around €1,600, and a 3-bed around €2,000. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Raheny is generally a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular DART and bus services, as well as cycle lanes and pedestrian-friendly streets, getting around Raheny is a breeze. The city centre is just a 20-minute DART ride away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bram Stoker, author of Dracula, was a Raheny resident? And don't miss the secret garden in St. Anne's Park - it's a real hidden gem!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its mix of old and new properties and ongoing developments, Raheny is an area on the rise. Yet it manages to retain its unique charm and community spirit.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers market in St. Anne's Park to the annual Raheny Festival, there's always something happening in Raheny.
