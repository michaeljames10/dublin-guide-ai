Welcome to Sandycove, Dublin's Seaside Gem

History

From its humble beginnings as a fishing village, Sandycove has evolved into a charming seaside neighbourhood. It's best known for the Martello Tower, where James Joyce once lived and set the opening scene of his famous novel, Ulysses.

Attractions

Don't miss the James Joyce Tower and Museum, the Forty Foot swimming spot, and the beautiful Sandycove Beach. The area is also a stone's throw away from the bustling town of Dún Laoghaire.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, locals recommend Cavistons Seafood Restaurant and the Glasthule Bakery. For a pint, you can't beat Fitzgerald's of Sandycove.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll along the coast or a picnic in Otranto Park. If you're up for a longer walk, the Sandycove to Dalkey cliff walk offers stunning views.

Local Life

Sandycove is a tight-knit community with a relaxed seaside vibe. It's a place where everyone knows their neighbours and children play freely in the streets.

Schools

The area is home to several excellent schools, including Harold National School and Rathdown School, a prestigious all-girls institution.

House Prices

House prices in Sandycove are on the higher end, reflecting the area's desirability and proximity to the sea.

Rent Prices

Rents are also above the Dublin average, with typical prices for 1-bed, 2-bed, and 3-bed places being €1,500, €2,000, and €2,500 respectively. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Sandycove is a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Locals get around by DART, bus, bike, or on foot. The city centre is just a 30-minute DART ride away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Sandycove hosts the annual Bloomsday celebration in honour of James Joyce? And don't miss the secret garden behind the James Joyce Tower!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Sandycove has largely retained its old-world charm, there are plans for further development of the Dún Laoghaire seafront.

Local Events & Markets

Check out the Glasthule Farmers Market every Sunday, and the annual Christmas swim at the Forty Foot.
