Welcome to Churchtown: A Charming Dublin Suburb with a Rich History and Vibrant Community

History

Churchtown's history dates back to the 13th century, with its name derived from the old church tower that still stands today. Over the centuries, it has evolved from a rural village to a bustling suburb, retaining its unique charm and heritage.

Attractions

From the historic Churchtown Castle to the bustling Dundrum Town Centre, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the Bottle Tower, a quirky local landmark.

Restaurants & Pubs

Churchtown boasts a variety of eateries and pubs. Locals recommend the cosy Churchtown Stores pub and the delicious Italian fare at Bellagio.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at the beautiful Marlay Park or take a stroll along the Dodder River. The area is also home to several golf clubs.

Local Life

Churchtown has a friendly, community-oriented vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

Churchtown is home to several excellent primary and secondary schools, including the well-regarded De La Salle College.

House Prices

House prices in Churchtown are generally above the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and excellent amenities.

Commuting & Transport

Churchtown is well-served by Luas, Dart, and buses. It's also a great area for cycling and walking, with the city centre just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Discover the secret garden at the Bottle Tower or the legendary ghost of Churchtown Castle. And don't forget to try the famous Churchtown Sausage!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Churchtown continues to be a sought-after area, with ongoing regeneration projects adding to its appeal.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Churchtown Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Churchtown.
