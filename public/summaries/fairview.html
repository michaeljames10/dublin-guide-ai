Welcome to Fairview, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a quiet fishing village, Fairview has evolved into a vibrant, bustling neighbourhood. Its rich history is evident in the charming Georgian and Victorian architecture that dots the area.

Attractions

From the iconic Fairview Park to the Marino Casino, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the local art galleries and the stunning views from the Clontarf seafront.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of the black stuff, Fairview's got you covered. Locals recommend The Bram Stoker for a cosy pub experience and Da Mimmo for the best Italian in town.

Green Spaces

Fairview is home to some of Dublin's best parks. Fairview Park is a local favourite, with its beautiful rose garden and bustling weekend farmers' market.

Local Life

Life in Fairview is a blend of old and new, with a strong sense of community and a vibrant local culture. It's a place where everyone knows your name and there's always a friendly face to greet you.

Schools

Fairview boasts a number of excellent primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded St. Joseph's CBS. Marino College is also a notable institution in the area.

House Prices

House prices in Fairview are generally more affordable than in other parts of Dublin, with a mix of charming period homes and modern apartments.

With excellent bus links, cycleways, and walkability, getting around Fairview is a breeze. The city centre is just a short commute away.

From the secret garden at Marino Casino to the legendary ghost of Bram Stoker's Dracula, Fairview is full of surprises.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its blend of historic charm and modern amenities, Fairview is an up-and-coming area that's attracting a lot of interest from homebuyers and investors alike.
