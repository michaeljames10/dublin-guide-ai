Welcome to Dalkey, Dublin's Charming Coastal Gem

History

From its roots as a Viking settlement to its evolution into a bustling seaside town, Dalkey's history is as rich as its character. Known for its medieval castles and Victorian architecture, the area has a unique heritage that's palpable in its streets.

Attractions

Visit Dalkey Castle, take a boat trip to Dalkey Island, or simply stroll along the scenic coastline. There's no shortage of things to do in this charming neighbourhood.

Restaurants & Pubs

From the cosy interiors of Finnegan's to the gourmet delights at DeVille's, Dalkey offers a variety of dining and drinking options. Locals recommend the seafood at Ouzos and a pint at The Queens.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a walk in Killiney Hill Park, take a dip at White Rock Beach, or explore the nature trails at Dalkey Quarry. The area is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts.

Local Life

Living in Dalkey is like being on a permanent holiday. The community is friendly, the pace is relaxed, and the sea is never far away.

Schools

Dalkey is home to several excellent primary and secondary schools, including Loreto Abbey and Harold Boys' National School.

House Prices

With its coastal views and village charm, Dalkey is one of Dublin's most desirable areas. House prices reflect this, with the average home costing significantly more than the city average.

With regular DART services and several bus routes, getting around is easy. The city centre is just a 30-minute train ride away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the secret tunnels of Dalkey Island to the ghost of Goat Castle, the area is full of quirky stories and hidden gems.

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Dalkey retains its village charm, it's also evolving, with new restaurants and shops adding to its appeal.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Dalkey Lobster Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in the area.
