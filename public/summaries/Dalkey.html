Welcome to Dalkey, Dublin's Charming Coastal Gem

History

Once a bustling medieval port, Dalkey has evolved into a charming coastal suburb with a rich history. From its 10th-century beginnings to its Victorian heyday, Dalkey's heritage is etched into its picturesque streets and historic castles.

Attractions

From the stunning Dalkey Island to the historic Dalkey Castle, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the scenic walks along Killiney Hill for breathtaking views of Dublin Bay.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty pub meal at Finnegan's or fine dining at DeVille's, Dalkey has you covered. Locals recommend the fresh seafood at Ouzos and a pint at The Queens.

Green Spaces

Dalkey is blessed with beautiful green spaces. Enjoy a picnic in Sorrento Park, a stroll along Vico Road, or a hike up Killiney Hill.

Local Life

Life in Dalkey is laid-back and friendly, with a strong sense of community. It's a place where everyone knows your name, and the sea is never more than a stone's throw away.

Schools

Dalkey boasts excellent primary and secondary schools, including Loreto Abbey and Harold Boys' National School. The area is also home to the renowned Dalkey School Project National School.

House Prices

With its coastal charm and village vibe, Dalkey is a sought-after area. Expect to pay a premium for property here, with prices typically above the Dublin average.

Rent Prices

Rents in Dalkey can be steep, with a 1-bed averaging €1,800, a 2-bed €2,500, and a 3-bed €3,200. Compared to the Dublin average, Dalkey is less affordable. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Dalkey is generally safe and peaceful, with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular DART services and several bus routes, getting around is easy. Dalkey is also a great place for walking and cycling, with plenty of scenic routes.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the secret tunnels of Dalkey Island to the ghost of Goat Castle, Dalkey is full of surprises. And let's not forget its famous residents, including Bono and Van Morrison!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Dalkey retains its timeless charm, there's always something new on the horizon. Keep an eye out for tasteful developments and property trends.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Dalkey Lobster Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening. And don't miss the local GAA matches!
