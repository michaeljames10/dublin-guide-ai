Welcome to Ballybough: Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Known as 'The Back of the Wall', Ballybough has a rich history dating back to the Viking era. It's seen its fair share of ups and downs, but has always retained its unique character and charm.

Attractions

From the iconic Croke Park Stadium to the tranquil Royal Canal, there's plenty to see and do in Ballybough. Don't miss the local street art, which tells the story of the area's past and present.

Green Spaces

Despite its urban location, Ballybough is blessed with plenty of green spaces. Fairview Park is a local favourite, with its beautiful rose garden and bustling weekend market.

Local Life

Ballybough is a close-knit community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name and there's always a local event to look forward to.

Schools

There are several good primary and secondary schools in the area, including St. Joseph's Co-Ed School and O'Connell's Secondary School. The Marino Institute of Education is also nearby.
