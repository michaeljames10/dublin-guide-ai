Welcome to Ballybough: The Heart of Dublin's Northside

History

Known as 'The Back of the Wall', Ballybough has a rich history dating back to the 12th century. It's seen everything from Viking invasions to the rise of the Celtic Tiger. The area's heritage is reflected in its charming Georgian architecture and the iconic Croke Park Stadium, home to Ireland's beloved Gaelic games.

Attractions

From the historic Croke Park to the Royal Canal, Ballybough is brimming with attractions. Don't miss the James Joyce Cultural Centre, a must-visit for literature buffs. For a taste of local life, stroll down the vibrant North Strand Road.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a hearty meal, head to The Yacht, a local favourite known for its traditional Irish fare. Fancy a pint? The Bridge Tavern is a classic Dublin pub, complete with a cosy fireplace and friendly locals.

Green Spaces

Despite its urban setting, Ballybough boasts several green spaces. Fairview Park, with its beautiful tree-lined paths, is perfect for a leisurely walk. The nearby Clontarf Promenade offers stunning sea views.

Local Life

Ballybough is a tight-knit community with a strong sense of pride. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name and local businesses thrive. The area's personality is best described as down-to-earth and welcoming.

Schools

Ballybough is home to several reputable schools, including O'Connell's Primary and Mount Carmel Secondary School. The area also boasts a number of notable institutions, such as the Marino Institute of Education.

House Prices

House prices in Ballybough are generally more affordable than the Dublin average, with a mix of terraced houses and modern apartments. The area has seen steady growth in recent years, making it a smart investment.

Commuting & Transport

With several bus routes and close proximity to Connolly Station, getting around from Ballybough is a breeze. The area is also highly walkable, with the city centre just a 20-minute stroll away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know Ballybough was once home to Bram Stoker, author of Dracula? Or that the area's beloved 'Banjo' pub got its name from a local legend who played the banjo? Discover these stories and more as you explore Ballybough's hidden gems.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With ongoing regeneration projects and a growing popularity among young professionals, Ballybough is definitely an area on the rise. Yet, it manages to retain its unique charm and community spirit.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Croke Park Classic to the bustling North Strand Road Market, there's always something happening in Ballybough. The area is also known for its vibrant GAA scene, with local matches drawing crowds from across Dublin.
