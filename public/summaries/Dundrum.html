Welcome to Dundrum: Your Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Suburb

History

Once a quiet village, Dundrum has evolved into a bustling suburb with a rich history. From the ancient ruins of Dundrum Castle to the modern Dundrum Town Centre, the area's heritage is a blend of old and new.

Attractions

Don't miss the Dundrum Town Centre, Ireland's largest shopping centre. For history buffs, the ruins of Dundrum Castle are a must-see. And for theatre lovers, the Mill Theatre Dundrum offers a variety of performances.

Restaurants & Pubs

From the rustic charm of The Eagle to the modern flair of Siam Thai, Dundrum offers a variety of dining options. For a pint, locals recommend The Dundrum House.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll in the beautiful Ballawley Park or explore the nature trails in Marlay Park. For outdoor activities, the Ticknock Mountain Bike Trail is a popular choice.

Local Life

Dundrum is a vibrant community with a friendly vibe. It's a place where modern amenities meet village charm, creating a unique personality that's distinctly Dundrum.

Schools

Dundrum is home to several excellent schools, including Taney Parish Primary School and Wesley College. The area also boasts the renowned Mount Anville Secondary School.

House Prices

House prices in Dundrum are generally on the higher side, reflecting the area's popularity and amenities. Expect to pay a premium for the privilege of living in this desirable suburb.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Dundrum are above the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,500 for a 1-bed, €2,000 for a 2-bed, and €2,500 for a 3-bed. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Dundrum is generally safe, with a crime index lower than the Dublin average. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With the Luas Green Line, numerous bus routes, and cycleways, getting around Dundrum is a breeze. The city centre is just a 20-minute Luas ride away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Discover the secret garden at Airfield Estate, or enjoy a pint at The Goat, a pub that's been serving locals since the 18th century. And don't forget to look out for the ghost of Dundrum Castle!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With ongoing developments like the Dundrum Central project, Dundrum is a suburb on the rise. Yet, it retains its charming village feel, making it a desirable place to live.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly Farmers Market to the annual Dundrum Festival, there's always something happening in Dundrum. And don't miss the Christmas lights switch-on, a local tradition.
