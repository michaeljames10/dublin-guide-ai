Welcome to Dundrum: Your Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Vibrant Suburb

History

Once a quiet village, Dundrum has evolved into a bustling suburb with a rich history. From the ancient ruins of Dundrum Castle to the modern Dundrum Town Centre, the area has seen significant growth and development over the centuries.

Attractions

Whether you're a history buff, a shopaholic, or a nature lover, Dundrum has something for everyone. Don't miss the Dundrum Town Centre, Ireland's largest shopping centre, and the beautiful Dundrum Castle.

Restaurants & Pubs

From traditional Irish pubs to trendy eateries, Dundrum offers a variety of dining options. Locals recommend the Goat's Gruff for a pint and the Gourmet Food Parlour for a hearty meal.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the great outdoors at Marlay Park or take a leisurely stroll along the Dodder River. Dundrum is also home to several golf courses and sports clubs.

Local Life

Dundrum is a vibrant community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. The area is known for its lively arts scene, with regular events and festivals throughout the year.

Schools

Dundrum boasts several top-rated primary and secondary schools, including Taney Parish Primary School and Dundrum College of Further Education.

House Prices

House prices in Dundrum are generally higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's popularity and desirability.
