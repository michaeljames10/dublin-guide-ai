Welcome to Clondalkin: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Clondalkin has a rich history dating back to the 7th century, with the iconic Round Tower as a testament to its early Christian heritage. The area has evolved over the centuries, with a mix of old and new, making it a unique spot in Dublin.

Attractions

From the Round Tower Visitor Centre to the Happy Pear café, Clondalkin has plenty to offer. Don't miss the Clondalkin Paper Mills, a testament to the area's industrial past.

Restaurants & Pubs

Clondalkin is home to some of Dublin's best local eateries and pubs. Try the hearty fare at The Laurels or enjoy a pint at Quinlan's.

Green Spaces

With Corkagh Park and the Grand Canal Greenway, Clondalkin offers plenty of green spaces for outdoor activities and nature walks.

Local Life

Clondalkin has a strong community vibe, with a mix of families, young professionals, and older residents. The area is known for its friendly locals and vibrant community events.

Schools

Clondalkin has a range of primary and secondary schools, including the highly regarded Coláiste Bríde and Scoil Íde.

House Prices

House prices in Clondalkin are generally more affordable than the Dublin average, with a mix of older homes and new developments.

Rent Prices

Crime Index

Commuting & Transport

Clondalkin is well-served by public transport, with Luas, Dart, and bus services. The area is also bike-friendly and walkable, with the city centre just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the annual Clondalkin Festival to the legendary ghost of Drimnagh Castle, Clondalkin is full of quirky stories and hidden gems.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Clondalkin is an up-and-coming area, with new developments and regeneration projects adding to its appeal.

Local Events & Markets

Clondalkin hosts regular community events, pop-up food markets, and sports traditions, making it a vibrant place to live.
