Welcome to Clontarf, Dublin's Coastal Gem

History

Clontarf's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From the Battle of Clontarf in 1014 to its evolution into a seaside suburb, this area has seen it all. It's even been home to the High King of Ireland, Brian Boru!

Attractions

From the stunning Clontarf Castle to the picturesque promenade, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the Bull Island Nature Reserve, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve teeming with wildlife.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, try the seafood at Fishbone or the Italian fare at Picasso. Fancy a pint? The Sheds pub is a local favourite, known for its cosy atmosphere and friendly staff.

Green Spaces

Clontarf is blessed with green spaces. St Anne's Park is a local gem, with its rose garden and weekend market. The seafront promenade is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a brisk jog.

Local Life

Life in Clontarf is laid-back and friendly. It's a tight-knit community with a village feel, despite being just a stone's throw from Dublin city centre.

Schools

Clontarf boasts excellent schools, including Belgrove Boys and Girls National School, Greenlanes National School, and Mount Temple Comprehensive.

House Prices

House prices in Clontarf can be steep, reflecting the area's desirability. Expect to pay upwards of €500,000 for a family home.

Rent Prices

Rents in Clontarf are higher than the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,500 for a 1-bed, €2,000 for a 2-bed, and €2,500 for a 3-bed. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Clontarf is a safe area with a low crime rate. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Clontarf is well-served by public transport, with regular bus services and the DART train line. It's also a great area for cycling, with dedicated cycle lanes along the seafront.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bram Stoker, author of Dracula, was a Clontarf local? Or that the area is home to the oldest cricket club in Ireland?

Property Trends & Future Developments

Clontarf continues to be a sought-after area, with property prices steadily rising. The area retains its charm while benefiting from ongoing improvements to local amenities.

Local Events & Markets

Check out the weekly farmers market in St Anne's Park, or the annual Clontarf Viking Festival. The local GAA club, Clontarf GAA, also hosts regular matches and events.
