Welcome to Clontarf, Dublin's Coastal Gem

History

Clontarf's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From the Battle of Clontarf in 1014, where the High King of Ireland Brian Boru defeated the Vikings, to its evolution into a seaside suburb in the 19th century, Clontarf has always been a place of significance.

Attractions

From the historic Clontarf Castle to the picturesque promenade, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the Bull Island Nature Reserve, a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve teeming with wildlife.

Restaurants & Pubs

Clontarf boasts a variety of eateries and watering holes. Try the seafood at Bay Restaurant, or enjoy a pint at the Yacht Pub, a local favourite.

Green Spaces

With St Anne's Park and the aforementioned Bull Island, Clontarf is a haven for nature lovers. There's also a great cycle path along the seafront.

Local Life

Life in Clontarf is laid-back and friendly. It's a tight-knit community with a village feel, despite being just a stone's throw from Dublin city centre.

Schools

Clontarf is home to several excellent schools, including Belgrove Boys School, Greenlanes National School, and Mount Temple Comprehensive, which famously educated the members of U2.

Commuting & Transport

Clontarf is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes and the DART train line. It's also a great area for cycling and walking.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bram Stoker, author of Dracula, was a Clontarf native? Or that the area is home to the oldest cricket club in Ireland?

Property Trends & Future Developments

Clontarf continues to be a sought-after area, with property prices steadily rising. The area maintains its charm while welcoming new developments.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers market in St Anne's Park to the annual Clontarf Viking Festival, there's always something happening.
