Welcome to Coolock, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Originally a small rural village, Coolock has grown into a bustling suburban hub. It's steeped in history, with roots dating back to the Viking era. The area has evolved over the centuries, with a rich tapestry of events shaping its unique character.

Attractions

From the Northside Shopping Centre to the Stardust Memorial Park, Coolock is packed with local landmarks and interesting spots. Don't miss the Odeon Cinema, a favourite among locals.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, locals recommend the Gourmet Food Parlour. If you fancy a pint, head to the Priorswood Inn, a traditional Irish pub with a friendly atmosphere.

Green Spaces

Coolock is home to several parks and green spaces, including the popular Stardust Memorial Park. It's a great spot for a nature walk or a picnic.

Local Life

Life in Coolock is laid-back and community-focused. The area has a strong sense of identity, with a vibrant mix of cultures and generations.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in Coolock, including the well-regarded Mercy College. The area is also home to the Northside Partnership, a notable community organisation.

Commuting & Transport

Getting around Coolock is easy, with several bus routes serving the area. The city centre is just a short bus ride away, and there are also plenty of cycleways and pedestrian-friendly streets.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

One of Coolock's best-kept secrets is the beautiful Santry River, a hidden gem that's perfect for a peaceful walk. The area is also home to several local legends, including the famous singer Bob Geldof.
