Welcome to Dartry, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a rural village, Dartry has evolved into a charming residential area. It's home to the historic Dartry House and the River Dodder, which has played a significant role in the area's development.

Attractions

From the picturesque Palmerston Park to the iconic Observatory, there's plenty to see in Dartry. Don't miss the local theatre and the historic mills along the river.

Restaurants & Pubs

Enjoy a pint at the local pub, or dine at one of the many restaurants offering everything from traditional Irish fare to international cuisine. Locals recommend the seafood chowder and the Guinness stew.

Green Spaces

Dartry is blessed with beautiful parks and nature walks. The River Dodder offers a scenic route for walkers and cyclists, and Palmerston Park is a favourite spot for picnics.

Local Life

Living in Dartry is like being in a village within the city. The community is tight-knit, and the area has a relaxed, friendly vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name.

Schools

Dartry boasts excellent primary and secondary schools. The area is also home to the prestigious Alexandra College, known for its high academic standards.

House Prices

House prices in Dartry are on the higher end, reflecting the area's desirability and the quality of its homes. Expect to pay a premium for the privilege of living here.

Rent Prices

Rents in Dartry are above the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,500 for a 1-bed, €2,000 for a 2-bed, and €2,500 for a 3-bed place. Affordability rating: 💸💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Dartry is a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Getting around is easy with the Luas Green Line and several bus routes serving the area. Dartry is also a great place for walking and cycling, with plenty of cycleways and pedestrian-friendly streets.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Dartry was once home to George Bernard Shaw? And don't miss the secret garden behind the old mill – it's a true hidden gem!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Dartry has retained its charming village feel, it's also embracing the future with new developments and renovations. It's an area that's both preserving its heritage and looking forward.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers' market to the annual Dartry Festival, there's always something happening in the area. And don't miss the local GAA matches – they're a real community event!
