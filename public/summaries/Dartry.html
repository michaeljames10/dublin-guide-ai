Welcome to Dartry, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a rural village, Dartry has evolved into a charming residential area, rich in history and heritage. It's home to the historic Dartry Mills and the famous Palmerston Park.

Attractions

From the picturesque River Dodder to the iconic Observatory Lane, there's plenty to see in Dartry. Don't miss the stunning architecture of the Church of the Three Patrons.

Restaurants & Pubs

Whether you're after a hearty Irish stew or a pint of the black stuff, Dartry's got you covered. Locals recommend the cosy atmosphere of The Dropping Well pub.

Green Spaces

With Palmerston Park on your doorstep and the Dodder River Walk nearby, Dartry is a haven for nature lovers. Perfect for a Sunday stroll or a picnic with the family.

Local Life

Dartry is a quiet, family-friendly area with a strong sense of community. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

Home to some of Dublin's top primary and secondary schools, including Alexandra College and Gonzaga College, Dartry is a great place for families with children.

House Prices

House prices in Dartry tend to be higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and excellent amenities.

With regular buses to the city centre and the Luas Green Line nearby, commuting from Dartry is a breeze. It's also a great area for cycling and walking.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Dartry was once home to George Bernard Shaw? And don't miss the secret garden at the end of Observatory Lane!

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Dartry retains its charming character, there's also exciting new development happening, with a range of modern apartments and townhouses on the horizon.
