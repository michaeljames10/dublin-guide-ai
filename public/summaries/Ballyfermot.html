Welcome to Ballyfermot: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Founded in the 1940s, Ballyfermot has a rich history rooted in community spirit. From its humble beginnings as a housing estate, it has grown into a vibrant neighbourhood with a unique character.

Attractions

Visit the Ballyfermot Heritage Group for a taste of local history, or take a stroll along the Grand Canal for some scenic views. Don't miss the Cherry Orchard Equine Centre, a haven for horse lovers.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a hearty meal, head to the Ballyfermot Cafe. If you fancy a pint, The Black Forge Inn is a local favourite.

Green Spaces

Markievicz Park is the perfect spot for a picnic, while the Grand Canal offers a tranquil setting for a leisurely walk.

Local Life

Ballyfermot is a close-knit community with a friendly vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in the area, including the well-regarded Ballyfermot College of Further Education.

House Prices

House prices in Ballyfermot are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it an affordable option for first-time buyers.

With regular bus services and easy access to the M50, getting around is a breeze. The city centre is just a 20-minute drive away.

Did you know that U2's Larry Mullen Jr. grew up in Ballyfermot? And don't forget to check out the Ballyfermot Duck Pond, a local secret!

With new developments on the horizon, Ballyfermot is an area on the rise. Yet it retains its charm and community spirit.
