Welcome to Walkinstown: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Walkinstown, a suburb of Dublin, has a rich history dating back to the 13th century. It was originally a small village surrounded by farmland, but it has evolved into a bustling residential area with a vibrant community spirit.

Attractions

From the historic Walkinstown Roundabout to the Drimnagh Castle, there's plenty to see and do in Walkinstown. Don't miss the Walkinstown Library, a hub of local culture and learning.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a taste of local cuisine, head to The Cherry Tree Pub or The Halfway House. Both offer a warm welcome and hearty Irish fare. For a more international flavour, try the Bombay Pantry for top-notch Indian cuisine.

Green Spaces

Walkinstown is home to several beautiful parks, including Walkinstown Park and Tymon Park. These green spaces are perfect for a leisurely stroll, a picnic, or a game of football.

Local Life

Life in Walkinstown is friendly and community-oriented. The area has a strong sense of local identity, with many residents involved in community groups and local events.

Schools

Walkinstown boasts several excellent primary and secondary schools, including Drimnagh Castle Primary and Greenhills College. The area is also home to the renowned Walkinstown Community School.

House Prices

House prices in Walkinstown are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it an affordable option for first-time buyers. The area offers a mix of property types, from traditional terraced houses to modern apartments.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Walkinstown are also below the Dublin average. Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Walkinstown is a safe and secure area, with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Walkinstown is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes passing through the area. It's also a short drive from the city centre, and there are plenty of cycle paths and pedestrian-friendly routes.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Walkinstown is full of hidden gems, from the quirky Walkinstown Green Shopping Centre to the legendary Cherry Tree Pub. And don't forget to look out for the famous Walkinstown Pigeon House!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Walkinstown is a stable and established area, but there are exciting developments on the horizon, including new housing projects and community facilities.

Local Events & Markets

Walkinstown hosts a range of local events and markets, from the annual Walkinstown Festival to the popular Greenhills Market. There's always something happening in this vibrant community.
