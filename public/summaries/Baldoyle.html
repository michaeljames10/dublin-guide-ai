Welcome to Baldoyle: A Local's Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a Viking settlement, Baldoyle has a rich history that's as colourful as its present. From its roots as a farming village to its evolution into a bustling suburb, Baldoyle's heritage is woven into the fabric of Dublin's story.

Attractions

From the stunning Baldoyle Racecourse to the tranquil coast, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the historic St. Doulagh's Church, one of Ireland's oldest stone-roofed churches still in use.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a pint and a chat, head to The Elphin or The White Sands Hotel. For food, locals swear by the fish and chips at Beshoff Bros or the Italian fare at Il Panorama Cafe.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll along the coast, a picnic in Racecourse Park, or a nature walk in the nearby Baldoyle Estuary. The area is also home to several golf clubs for the sporty types.

Local Life

Baldoyle is a friendly, community-oriented area with a laid-back vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in the area, including Baldoyle Secondary School and St. Peter and Paul's Junior School. The area is also close to Dublin City University.

House Prices

House prices in Baldoyle are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a mix of older homes and new developments.

Rent Prices

Rents for a 1-bed start around €1,200, 2-beds at €1,500, and 3-beds at €1,800. Compared to the Dublin average, Baldoyle offers good value for money. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Baldoyle is generally a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular DART and bus services, getting around is easy. The area is also very walkable and has several cycle paths. Commute times to the city centre are typically 30-40 minutes.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Baldoyle was once home to Bram Stoker, author of Dracula? Or that the area is said to be haunted by a friendly ghost named Molly?

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Baldoyle retains its charm, it's also seeing new developments, particularly near the coast. It's an area that's growing, but not losing its soul.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Baldoyle Mayfest to the weekly farmers market, there's always something happening. The area also has a strong sporting tradition, with local GAA and football clubs.

Area Vibe Score

Overall Vibe: A friendly, community-oriented suburb with a touch of coastal charm.
