Welcome to Baldoyle: A Local's Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a Viking settlement, Baldoyle has evolved into a charming suburban neighbourhood with a rich history. From its 19th-century horse racing track to the iconic St. Doulagh's Church, the area's heritage is as diverse as it is fascinating.

Attractions

Don't miss the Baldoyle Racecourse, a testament to the area's equestrian past, or the stunning views from the coastal walk. The local library is a hub of community activity, and the nearby Island Golf Club is a must-visit for golf enthusiasts.

Restaurants & Pubs

From the hearty fare at The White House to the cosy atmosphere of The Elphin, Baldoyle's pubs and restaurants are a treat. Locals recommend the seafood chowder at The Racecourse Inn and the Sunday roast at The Baldoyle House.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll in Racecourse Park, explore the biodiversity of Baldoyle Estuary, or take a leisurely bike ride along the coast. The area is also home to several sports clubs, offering everything from football to sailing.

Local Life

Baldoyle is a friendly, community-oriented neighbourhood with a relaxed vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name, and local events like the annual summer festival bring everyone together.

Schools

With several primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded Baldoyle Secondary School, the area is a great choice for families. The local community school also offers adult education classes.

House Prices

House prices in Baldoyle are generally in line with the Dublin average, with a mix of older, character-filled homes and modern developments. The area is popular with both first-time buyers and families looking for more space.

Commuting & Transport

With regular bus services, a DART station, and cycle-friendly routes, getting around Baldoyle is a breeze. The city centre is just a 30-minute commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

From the annual duck race at the local festival to the legendary ghost of the old racecourse, Baldoyle is full of quirky stories and hidden gems. And did you know that U2's Larry Mullen Jr. grew up here?

Property Trends & Future Developments

While Baldoyle retains its charming village feel, there's plenty of exciting development on the horizon, including new housing and community facilities. It's an area that's definitely on the up.
