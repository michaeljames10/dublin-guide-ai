Welcome to Castleknock: Your Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Castleknock's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From its origins as a Viking settlement to its growth during the Georgian era, this area has seen it all. The name Castleknock comes from the Irish Caisleán Cnucha, meaning 'Cnucha's Castle' - a nod to its ancient roots.

Attractions

From the historic Castleknock Castle to the Phoenix Park, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the Farmleigh House, a stunning Edwardian estate that's hosted everyone from Queen Elizabeth II to Nelson Mandela.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a bite to eat, try Myos Pub for traditional Irish fare or Okra Green for some of the best Pakistani cuisine in Dublin. And of course, no visit to Castleknock would be complete without a pint at The Carpenter, a local favourite.

Green Spaces

Castleknock is a green oasis in the heart of Dublin. Take a stroll through Phoenix Park, the largest enclosed public park in any European capital, or explore the Royal Canal Way for a peaceful waterside walk.

Local Life

Life in Castleknock is a blend of old and new, with a strong sense of community. It's a place where neighbours know each other by name and local businesses are cherished.

Schools

Castleknock boasts some of the best schools in Dublin, including Castleknock Community College and St. Brigid's National School. The area is also home to Castleknock College, a prestigious secondary school with a rich history.

House Prices

House prices in Castleknock tend to be higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and excellent amenities. Expect to pay a premium for properties close to Phoenix Park or the village centre.

Rent Prices

Rent prices in Castleknock are on the higher side, with typical rents for a 1-bed around €1,500, a 2-bed around €2,000, and a 3-bed around €2,500. Compared to the Dublin average, Castleknock is less affordable. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Castleknock is one of the safer areas in Dublin, with a low crime index. Safety: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Castleknock is well-served by public transport, with regular bus services and a train station. The area is also highly walkable, with many residents choosing to cycle or walk to work.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Bram Stoker, author of Dracula, was a Castleknock resident? Or that Phoenix Park is home to a herd of wild deer? Castleknock is full of surprises!

Property Trends & Future Developments

Castleknock continues to be a sought-after area, with property values steadily rising. The area is also seeing new developments, with a focus on sustainable living and community spaces.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Castleknock Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Castleknock. The area also has a strong sporting tradition, with local GAA and rugby clubs.
