Welcome to Phibsborough: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Phibsborough, also known as Phibsboro, is steeped in history. From its origins as a small village in the 18th century, it has grown into a bustling urban neighbourhood. It's home to the iconic Dalymount Park, the historic home of Bohemian FC, and the Royal Canal, a testament to Ireland's industrial past.

Attractions

There's plenty to see in Phibsborough. The Blessington Basin is a tranquil oasis, while the Mater Hospital's Eccles Street entrance is a must-see for James Joyce fans. And don't forget to check out the Phibsborough Library, a charming Victorian building.

Restaurants & Pubs

Phibsborough is a foodie's paradise. From the hearty fare at Woodstock Cafe to the delicious pizzas at Cotto, there's something for everyone. For a pint, you can't beat the cosy atmosphere of The Hut or McGowan's.

Green Spaces

Despite its urban setting, Phibsborough is blessed with green spaces. The Blessington Basin and the Royal Canal offer peaceful retreats, while the nearby Phoenix Park is perfect for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.

Local Life

Phibsborough is a vibrant, diverse neighbourhood with a strong sense of community. It's a place where you'll find students, families, and professionals living side by side, contributing to the area's unique charm.

Schools

Phibsborough boasts a number of excellent schools, including the multi-denominational Educate Together primary school and the highly-regarded St. Peter's College.

Commuting & Transport

Phibsborough is well-served by public transport, with numerous bus routes and the Luas Green Line nearby. It's also a great area for cycling, with plenty of bike lanes and Dublin Bikes stations.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Phibsborough was home to Bram Stoker, author of Dracula? Or that the Royal Canal was once a bustling trade route? Discover these stories and more as you explore the area.

Property Trends & Future Developments
