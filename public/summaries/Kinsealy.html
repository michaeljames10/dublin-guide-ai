Welcome to Kinsealy: A Local's Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a quiet farming village, Kinsealy has evolved into a vibrant suburb with a rich history. From the ancient ruins of St. Nicholas' Church to the 18th-century Kinsealy Court, the area's heritage is a testament to its enduring charm.

Attractions

Don't miss the Malahide Castle and Gardens, a stone's throw away. For golf enthusiasts, the Portmarnock Golf Club is a must-visit. And for a unique experience, check out the Fry Model Railway Museum.

Restaurants & Pubs

From the hearty fare at Gourmet Food Parlour to the traditional pub experience at The Kinsealy Inn, there's something for every palate. Locals recommend the seafood at Seabank Bistro.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a leisurely stroll in the lush Kinsealy Grange or explore the scenic trails of Malahide Demesne. For outdoor activities, the Robswall Park is a popular choice.

Local Life

Life in Kinsealy is a blend of suburban tranquility and city convenience. The community is tight-knit, friendly, and welcoming, making it a great place to call home.

Schools

Kinsealy boasts several excellent primary and secondary schools, including the highly-rated St. Nicholas' Primary School and Portmarnock Community School.

House Prices

House prices in Kinsealy are generally on par with the Dublin average, with a typical home costing around €400,000.

Rent Prices

Expect to pay around €1,200 for a 1-bed, €1,500 for a 2-bed, and €1,800 for a 3-bed place. Compared to the Dublin average, Kinsealy offers good value for money. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Kinsealy is a safe and secure neighbourhood with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

With regular bus services, close proximity to the Dart, and excellent cycleways, getting around is a breeze. The city centre is just a 30-minute commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Kinsealy was once home to Thin Lizzy's Phil Lynott? And don't miss the secret garden at the end of Chapel Road – a true hidden gem!

Property Trends & Future Developments

With ongoing developments and a growing community, Kinsealy is an up-and-coming area that retains its charming village feel.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers' market to the annual Kinsealy Festival, there's always something happening in the neighbourhood.

Area Vibe Score
