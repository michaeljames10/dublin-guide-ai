Welcome to Kinsealy: A Local's Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Once a quiet farming village, Kinsealy has evolved into a vibrant suburb with a rich history. It's home to the historic Kinsealy Court and the iconic St. Nicholas of Myra Church, both testaments to the area's deep-rooted heritage.

Attractions

From the tranquil Malahide Castle & Gardens to the bustling Kinsealy Pet Farm, there's something for everyone. Don't miss the Portmarnock Golf Club, a favourite among locals and tourists alike.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a hearty meal, head to the Kinsealy Grange Kitchen. Fancy a pint? The local's choice is Gibney's of Malahide, a traditional Irish pub with a warm, friendly atmosphere.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a leisurely stroll in the lush Malahide Demesne, or take a nature walk along the scenic Broadmeadow Estuary. For the more adventurous, there's the High Ropes Adventure Centre.

Local Life

Life in Kinsealy is a blend of rural charm and modern convenience. The community is tight-knit, with regular local events and a strong sense of camaraderie.

Schools

Kinsealy boasts several excellent primary and secondary schools, including the highly-regarded Portmarnock Community School and St. Nicholas of Myra National School.

Commuting & Transport

With regular bus services, cycleways, and a short drive to the nearest Dart station, getting around Kinsealy is a breeze. The city centre is just a 30-minute commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that the legendary Thin Lizzy frontman Phil Lynott once lived in Kinsealy? And don't forget to visit the secret garden at the end of Abbey Park Lane, a true hidden gem.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new developments on the horizon and a steady increase in property values, Kinsealy is definitely an area to watch.
