Welcome to Ashtown: A Local's Comprehensive Guide to Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Ashtown's history is as rich as a pint of Guinness. From its humble beginnings as a rural village, it has evolved into a vibrant, modern neighbourhood while still retaining its historical charm. Key events include the establishment of the Royal Canal, which brought prosperity to the area, and the opening of the Phoenix Park, one of Europe's largest city parks.

Attractions

Ashtown is home to several local landmarks, including the Ashtown Castle, a restored medieval tower house. The Royal Canal Way offers a scenic walk, and the Phoenix Park is a must-visit for nature lovers and history buffs alike.

Restaurants & Pubs

From traditional Irish pubs to modern eateries, Ashtown has it all. Locals recommend The Halfway House for a pint and a chat, and My Meat Wagon for some of the best BBQ in Dublin.

Green Spaces

Ashtown is blessed with an abundance of green spaces. The Phoenix Park is a local favourite, offering vast open spaces, woodland walks, and even a zoo. The Royal Canal Way is perfect for a leisurely stroll or cycle.

Local Life

Life in Ashtown is a blend of the traditional and the modern. The community is friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of local pride. The area has a relaxed, village-like vibe, but with all the amenities of city life.

Schools

Ashtown boasts several excellent primary and secondary schools. Notable institutions include the Pelletstown Educate Together National School and St. John Bosco's Boys' School.

House Prices

House prices in Ashtown are generally more affordable than in central Dublin, with a mix of traditional and modern homes available. The area is popular with families and first-time buyers.

Commuting & Transport

Ashtown is well-served by public transport, with regular Luas and Dart services. The area is also very walkable, and there are several cycleways for those who prefer to travel on two wheels. Commute times to the city centre are typically around 30 minutes.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Ashtown is full of hidden gems and quirky stories. Don't miss the secret garden at the Phoenix Park, and be sure to visit the local legend that is Nancy Hands, one of Dublin's oldest pubs.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Ashtown is an up-and-coming area, with several new developments planned. However, the area is committed to preserving its unique character and charm.

Local Events & Markets

Ashtown has a vibrant community spirit, with regular events and markets. Highlights include the Ashtown Village Market and the annual Phoenix Park Summer Festival.
