Welcome to Drimnagh, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Originally a small rural village, Drimnagh has grown into a bustling residential area with a rich history. It's home to the historic Drimnagh Castle, one of the few remaining castles in Ireland with a flooded moat.

Attractions

From the medieval Drimnagh Castle to the modern Our Lady's Children's Hospital, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the local GAA club, a hub of community activity and Gaelic sports.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a pint and a chat, head to The Gate Bar. If you're peckish, try the local favourite, the Golden Chip for some of the best fish and chips in Dublin.

Green Spaces

Brickfield Park is a local favourite for a stroll or a kickabout. The Grand Canal also runs through Drimnagh, offering a scenic waterside walk.

Local Life

Drimnagh is a close-knit community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in Drimnagh, including Drimnagh Castle Primary and Our Lady of Good Counsel Boys School.

House Prices

House prices in Drimnagh are generally more affordable than the Dublin average, with a mix of older terraced homes and newer developments.

Commuting & Transport

Drimnagh is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes and the Luas Red Line providing easy access to the city centre. It's also a great area for cycling, with plenty of cycle lanes.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Drimnagh Castle is said to be haunted? Or that the local GAA club has produced several All-Ireland winners? There's always something new to discover in Drimnagh.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its affordable housing and strong community, Drimnagh is an increasingly popular choice for homebuyers. The area is also seeing some exciting new developments, including a planned upgrade of the local park.

Local Events & Markets

From the annual Drimnagh Festival to the weekly farmers' market, there's always something happening in Drimnagh. And don't miss the local GAA matches, a true Dublin experience.
