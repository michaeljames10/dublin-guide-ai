Welcome to Drimnagh, Dublin's Hidden Gem

History

Originally a small rural village, Drimnagh has grown into a bustling residential area with a rich history. It's home to the historic Drimnagh Castle, the only remaining castle in Ireland with a flooded moat.

Attractions

From the medieval Drimnagh Castle to the modern Our Lady's Children's Hospital, there's plenty to see and do in Drimnagh. Don't miss the local GAA club, a hub of community activity.

Restaurants & Pubs

For a pint and a chat, head to The Gate Bar. If you're peckish, try the local favourite, The Hungry Duck, known for its hearty breakfasts and lunches.

Green Spaces

Brickfield Park is a local favourite for a stroll or a kickabout. The Grand Canal also offers a lovely waterside walk.

Local Life

Drimnagh is a close-knit community with a friendly, welcoming vibe. It's a place where neighbours know each other and local businesses thrive.

Schools

There are several good primary and secondary schools in Drimnagh, including Drimnagh Castle Primary and Secondary Schools, both located in the historic castle grounds.

House Prices

House prices in Drimnagh are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.

Commuting & Transport

With the Luas Red Line running through Drimnagh, commuting to the city centre is a breeze. There are also several bus routes and cycle lanes, and the area is very walkable.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Drimnagh Castle is said to be haunted? Or that the local GAA club has produced several All-Ireland winners? There's always something new to discover in Drimnagh.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With its affordable housing and excellent transport links, Drimnagh is definitely an area on the rise. Watch this space!
