Welcome to Rush, Dublin's Hidden Seaside Gem

History

Once a quiet fishing village, Rush has evolved into a vibrant residential area while still retaining its historical charm. Known for its two beautiful beaches, it's also home to Kenure House, a grand mansion with a history dating back to the 16th century.

Attractions

From the Martello Tower to the Rush Harbour, there's plenty to see. Don't miss the annual Rush Harbour Festival, a local favourite!

Restaurants & Pubs

For a pint and a chat, head to The Harbour Bar. If you're peckish, try some fresh seafood at The Strand Bar & Restaurant.

Green Spaces

Enjoy a stroll along the North and South beaches, or explore the lush greenery of Kenure Woods.

Local Life

Rush is a friendly, close-knit community with a laid-back seaside vibe. It's the perfect place to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

Schools

There are several primary and secondary schools in the area, including St. Joseph's Secondary School, known for its strong community spirit.

House Prices

House prices in Rush are generally lower than the Dublin average, making it a popular choice for first-time buyers.

Rent Prices

Rents for a 1-bed, 2-bed, and 3-bed places are typically €1,200, €1,500, and €1,800 respectively. Compared to the Dublin average, Rush is quite affordable. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Rush is a safe area with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

While Rush doesn't have a Luas or Dart station, it's well-served by buses. The 33 and 33A routes provide regular service to Dublin city centre.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that Rush was once the heart of the Irish film industry? The old Lambay Studios are a testament to this forgotten era.

Property Trends & Future Developments

With new developments planned, Rush is an area on the rise. Yet, it remains a charming seaside town at heart.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly farmers' market to the annual Rush Harbour Festival, there's always something happening in Rush.
