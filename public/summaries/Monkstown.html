Welcome to the Charming Monkstown: Your Ultimate Local Guide

History

Monkstown, a charming seaside village, has a rich history dating back to the 8th century. It's named after a small monastic settlement and has evolved into a vibrant community, retaining its historical charm with Georgian and Victorian architecture.

Attractions

From the stunning Monkstown Parish Church to the bustling Monkstown Farmer's Market, there's plenty to see and do. Don't miss the Monkstown Castle, a hidden gem with a fascinating history.

Restaurants & Pubs

Monkstown is a foodie's paradise. Check out the local favourite, Goggins Pub, for a pint and traditional Irish fare. For a more upscale dining experience, visit Lobstar, known for its exquisite seafood.

Green Spaces

Enjoy the outdoors at the Monkstown Park, a perfect spot for a picnic or a leisurely stroll. The nearby coastline also offers beautiful walking trails with stunning sea views.

Local Life

Living in Monkstown is like stepping back in time, with its quaint village vibe and friendly community. It's a peaceful haven away from the hustle and bustle of Dublin city centre.

Schools

Monkstown boasts excellent schools, including Monkstown Park Junior School and CBC Monkstown, both known for their high academic standards and nurturing environment.

House Prices

House prices in Monkstown tend to be higher than the Dublin average, reflecting the area's desirability and charm. Expect to find a mix of period homes and modern apartments.

Rent Prices

Rents in Monkstown can be steep, with a 1-bed apartment averaging €1,500 per month, a 2-bed around €2,000, and a 3-bed upwards of €2,500. Compared to the Dublin average, Monkstown is less affordable, but its charm and amenities make it worth the price. Affordability: 💸💸💸💸⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪⚪

Crime Index

Monkstown is a safe and secure neighbourhood, with a low crime index. Safety rating: 🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒🔒⚪⚪⚪

Commuting & Transport

Monkstown is well-served by public transport, including the DART and several bus routes. It's also a great area for cycling and walking, with the city centre just a short commute away.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

Did you know that famous writer James Joyce once lived in Monkstown? And don't miss the secret garden behind the Monkstown Parish Church, a true hidden gem.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Monkstown continues to be a sought-after area, with property values steadily increasing. The area maintains its charm while embracing modern amenities and developments.

Local Events & Markets

From the weekly Farmer's Market to the annual Monkstown International Puppet Festival, there's always something happening in Monkstown.
