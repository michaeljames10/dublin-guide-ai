Welcome to Knocklyon: A Hidden Gem in Dublin's Southside

History

Knocklyon is a suburb in the south of Dublin with a rich history. It was once a rural area, home to farms and country houses. The area has evolved over the years, with the development of housing estates in the 1970s and 80s, transforming it into the bustling suburb it is today.

Attractions

Knocklyon is home to several local landmarks, including the 12th-century St. Colmcille's Church, and the Knocklyon Shopping Centre. The area also boasts several walking trails and parks, perfect for a leisurely stroll or a family picnic.

Restaurants & Pubs

From traditional Irish pubs to modern eateries, Knocklyon has something for everyone. Locals recommend the hearty fare at The Speaker Connolly, and the delicious Italian cuisine at Bellagio.

Green Spaces

Knocklyon is known for its green spaces, including the beautiful Tymon Park, and the scenic walks along the Dodder River. These spaces offer a range of outdoor activities, from walking and cycling to bird watching and picnicking.

Local Life

Life in Knocklyon is a blend of suburban tranquility and city convenience. The community is friendly and welcoming, with a strong sense of local pride. The area has a relaxed, family-friendly vibe, with plenty of local events and activities to enjoy.

Schools

Knocklyon is home to several excellent primary and secondary schools, including St. Colmcille's Community School, one of the top-rated schools in the country.

House Prices

House prices in Knocklyon are generally in line with the Dublin average, with a typical three-bedroom home costing around €400,000.

Rent Prices

Commuting & Transport

Knocklyon is well-served by public transport, with several bus routes passing through the area. The M50 motorway is also easily accessible, making commuting to the city centre a breeze.

Hidden Gems & Local Legends

One of Knocklyon's hidden gems is the annual St. Colmcille's Festival, a community event that celebrates the area's history and heritage. The area is also home to several local legends, including the ghost of a 17th-century highwayman said to haunt the old coach road.

Property Trends & Future Developments

Knocklyon is a stable and desirable area, with property values expected to continue rising in the coming years. The area is also set to benefit from several planned developments, including a new community centre and park.

Local Events & Markets

Knocklyon hosts a range of local events and markets, including the popular Knocklyon Christmas Market, and the annual St. Colmcille's Festival.
